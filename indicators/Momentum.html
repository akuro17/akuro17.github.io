Momentum

PART 1: MAIN GUIDE

1. Identity

Full Name: Momentum Indicator

Abbreviation: MOM / MTM

Category: Momentum Oscillator

Creator: Traditional (foundational indicator)

One-Sentence Definition: Momentum is the simplest measure of price change velocity, calculated as the *difference* between the current price and the price N periods ago, showing how fast and in which direction price is moving.

2. Calculation Logic

Formula

The Momentum indicator is elegantly simple:

$$Momentum = Price_{current} - Price_{n\ periods\ ago}$$

VariableDefinition
$Price_{current}$Today's closing price
$Price_{n\ periods\ ago}$Closing price N bars back
$n$Lookback period (default: 10)

Example

DayCloseMomentum (10)
Day 10105105 - 95 = +10
Day 11103103 - 96 = +7
Day 12107107 - 97 = +10

Real-World Analogy

Momentum is like a car's speedometer reading. If you're at mile 105 today and you were at mile 95 an hour ago, your momentum is +10 mph. Unlike velocity (which could be calculated continuously), momentum just measures the straight-line displacement. Positive momentum means you're moving forward; negative means backward; the magnitude shows how fast.

Why This Simplicity?

Momentum is often the *first* indicator new traders learn. Its simplicity is its power—there's no smoothing, no weights, no complex formulas. The raw displacement tells you exactly how far price has moved.

7. Practical Cautions

Lag Characteristics

AspectCharacteristic
Calculation LagPeriod bars (by definition)
Signal LagNone—it's the raw difference
First Valid ValueAfter Period bars
Reality Check: Momentum is non-normalized. A stock priced at $500 will have much larger momentum values than one priced at $10. Use Rate of Change (ROC) for percentage-based normalization.

False Signal Scenarios

1. Base Effect

If the price N periods ago was unusually high (gap up), momentum will be artificially low today when that bar drops out.

2. Zero-Line Oscillation

In ranging markets, momentum crosses zero frequently without conviction.

3. Anchor Outlier

A flash crash N periods ago causes momentum to spike when that bar leaves the calculation.

Mitigation Strategies

- Use ROC Instead: ROC = (Price / Price[N]) - 1 gives percentage change

- Smooth Momentum: Apply 3-period SMA to reduce noise

- Confirm with Volume: Require volume spike on zero crossover

8. Pro Insights

Momentum vs. ROC

- Momentum: $Price - Price_{N\ periods\ ago}$ (absolute difference)

- ROC: $(Price / Price_{N\ periods\ ago}) - 1$ (percentage change)

Use Momentum when the *magnitude* matters (e.g., for the same stock over time). Use ROC when comparing across different assets or price levels.

Divergence Is King

Momentum divergence is one of the most reliable reversal signals:

- Price makes new high, Momentum makes lower high → Trend weakening

- Price makes new low, Momentum makes higher low → Selling exhausting

Momentum of Momentum

For a second-derivative signal, calculate momentum of momentum:

$MoM = Momentum_{today} - Momentum_{N\ periods\ ago}$

This shows *acceleration*—is momentum itself speeding up or slowing down?

9. Summary

"Momentum is the market's speedometer—positive means we're moving forward, negative means backward, and the magnitude tells you how fast we're traveling."

PART 2: SUPPLEMENTARY GUIDE

1. Core Concept

Momentum answers: "How far has price moved from where it was N periods ago?"

It's the purest form of velocity measurement—no smoothing, no weighting, no normalization. Just: Where are we now minus where were we then?

2. Statistical Edge

Why It Works

Markets exhibit momentum persistence. Studies show that assets rising tend to continue rising (short-term) due to behavioral factors like herding and delayed information diffusion. Momentum simply quantifies this effect.

Academic Reference

The "momentum factor" is one of the most researched anomalies in finance, documented in Jegadeesh & Titman's 1993 paper demonstrating that stocks with high past returns continue to outperform.

PART 3: ADVANCED SIGNAL REFERENCE

Signal Patterns

Momentum Zero Cross Signal

This signal fires when Momentum crosses above zero, indicating price is now higher than it was N periods ago. This is the simplest trend direction signal—positive momentum means upward velocity. The zero line is the neutral point; crossovers indicate directional shifts in price displacement.


def momentum_buy_signal(momentum, momentum_prev):
    zero_cross = momentum > 0 and momentum_prev <= 0
    return zero_cross

Momentum Bullish Divergence

This pattern detects when price makes a lower low but Momentum makes a higher low—a classic bullish divergence. Since Momentum is the "first derivative" of price, this divergence indicates deceleration in selling pressure. This often appears 2-5 bars before price bottoms, making it a powerful early warning signal.


def momentum_bullish_divergence(price_low, prev_price_low, mom_low, prev_mom_low):
    price_lower = price_low < prev_price_low
    mom_higher = mom_low > prev_mom_low
    return price_lower and mom_higher
