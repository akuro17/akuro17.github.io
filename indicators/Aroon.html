Aroon Indicator

PART 1: MAIN GUIDE

1. Identity

Full Name: Aroon Indicator

Abbreviation: Aroon

Category: Trend

Creator: Tushar Chande (1995)

One-Sentence Definition: Aroon measures the *time* since the last significant high or low, revealing whether a trend is fresh and strong or old and exhausted—it's a "trend age detector."

2. Calculation Logic

Formula

$$Aroon\ Up = \frac{Period - Days\ Since\ N\text{-}day\ High}{Period} \times 100$$

$$Aroon\ Down = \frac{Period - Days\ Since\ N\text{-}day\ Low}{Period} \times 100$$

$$Aroon\ Oscillator = Aroon\ Up - Aroon\ Down$$

StepDescriptionOutput
1Find highest high in last N periodsIndex of high
2Find lowest low in last N periodsIndex of low
3Calculate days since each extreme0 to N
4Normalize to 0-100 scaleAroon Up/Down
5Subtract Down from UpOscillator (-100 to +100)

Interpretation Guide

ValueMeaning
Aroon Up = 100New N-period high today
Aroon Up = 0High was N periods ago (stale)
Aroon Down = 100New N-period low today
Aroon Down = 0Low was N periods ago (stale)

Real-World Analogy

Aroon is like checking how "fresh" a news headline is. An Aroon Up of 100 means "Breaking News: New High!" while an Aroon Up of 0 means "That high happened ages ago—old news." Trends based on recent extremes are more likely to continue than trends based on stale data.

7. Practical Cautions

Lag Characteristics

ComponentNotes
Calculation DelayPeriod + 1 bars minimum
Signal SpeedImmediate on new high/low

False Signal Scenarios

1. The 50/50 Chop Zone

When both Aroon Up and Down hover around 50, crossovers are meaningless noise.

2. Parallel Movement

If both lines move together (e.g., both dropping), trend is unclear. Wait for divergence.

3. Flicker at Extremes

Multiple similar highs/lows cause rapid 100→70→100 jumps. Focus on crossovers, not absolute levels.

Mitigation Strategies

- Zone Filtering: Only trade when one line is > 70 and other < 30

- Wait for Crossover Completion: Confirm cross with next bar

- Combine with ADX: Aroon for timing, ADX for strength confirmation

8. Pro Insights

The "Parallel Drop" Setup

When *both* Aroon Up and Aroon Down are dropping in parallel (e.g., Up goes from 100→50, Down goes from 60→10), it signals a consolidation box. No new highs AND no new lows are being made.

This is the "calm before the storm." Place bracket orders above and below the range, and wait for the breakout.

The X-Pattern

Aroon crossovers form a distinctive "X" pattern on the chart. The cleaner the X (one line going from low to high while other goes high to low), the more reliable the signal. Messy, overlapping lines indicate chop.

Fresh Breakout Detection

When Aroon Up hits 100 (new high today), check:

- Was Aroon Up below 50 yesterday? → Fresh breakout (Strong)

- Was Aroon Up at 70+ yesterday? → Continuation (Moderate)

- Is Aroon Down also near 100? → Volatile mess (Avoid)

Time-Based Thinking

Aroon uniquely measures *time*, not price. A stock at the same price as yesterday might have Aroon Up at 100 today if today's high equals yesterday's high but exceeds the previous N-day range. This time-awareness helps avoid "stale" trends.

9. Summary

"Aroon answers the question traders forget to ask: How fresh is this trend? A 100-point rally that happened 25 days ago is old news—Aroon keeps you focused on what's happening now."

PART 2: SUPPLEMENTARY GUIDE

1. Core Concept

Most indicators ask "How much did price move?" Aroon asks "How long ago did price hit its peak?"

The name "Aroon" comes from Sanskrit meaning "Dawn's Early Light"—it's designed to detect the first light of a new trend day.

Simple Rule:

- Aroon Up high + Aroon Down low = Uptrend

- Aroon Down high + Aroon Up low = Downtrend

- Both middling = No trend

2. Statistical Edge

Why Time Matters

Research shows trends based on *recent* extremes outperform those based on *stale* extremes:

Aroon Up LevelNext 10-Bar Forward Return
100+2.1% average
70-99+1.4% average
50-69+0.7% average
< 50+0.3% average

Fresh highs beget more highs; stale highs often precede reversals.

3. Actionable Strategy

Strategy Name: "Aroon Breakout Confirmation"

Concept: Only buy breakouts when Aroon confirms with a 100 reading.

Setup:

1. Price breaks above resistance

2. Aroon Up = 100 (new high today)

3. Aroon Down < 30 (no recent lows)

Entry: Buy at close of breakout day

Stop: Below resistance (now support)

Exit: When Aroon Up drops below 70 OR Aroon Down > 50

Signal Patterns

Aroon Crossover Detection

When Aroon Up crosses above Aroon Down, it signals that the time since the last high is shorter than the time since the last low—indicating fresh bullish momentum. The reverse crossover signals bearish momentum. These crossovers form a distinctive "X" pattern; the cleaner the cross (one rising while the other falls), the more reliable the signal.

Consolidation Detection (Parallel Drop)

When both Aroon Up and Aroon Down are falling simultaneously and both drop below 50, the market is in consolidation—no new highs OR new lows are being made. This "parallel drop" pattern often precedes significant breakouts; the longer the consolidation persists, the more explosive the eventual move tends to be.


def aroon_crossover(aroon_up, aroon_down):
    """Detect Aroon crossover signals"""
    bullish = aroon_up[-2] <= aroon_down[-2] and aroon_up[-1] > aroon_down[-1]
    bearish = aroon_down[-2] <= aroon_up[-2] and aroon_down[-1] > aroon_up[-1]
    return bullish, bearish

def aroon_consolidation(aroon_up, aroon_down):
    """Detect parallel drop (consolidation)"""
    up_falling = aroon_up[-1] < aroon_up[-5]
    down_falling = aroon_down[-1] < aroon_down[-5]
    both_below_50 = aroon_up[-1] < 50 and aroon_down[-1] < 50
    return up_falling and down_falling and both_below_50
