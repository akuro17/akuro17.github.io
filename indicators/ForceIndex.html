Force Index

PART 1: MAIN GUIDE

1. Identity

Full Name: Elder's Force Index

Abbreviation: EFI

Category: Momentum / Volume / Oscillator

Creator: Dr. Alexander Elder (1993)

One-Sentence Definition: The Force Index measures the power behind price moves by combining direction, magnitude, and volume—revealing whether bulls or bears truly control the market.

2. Calculation Logic

Formula

StepFormulaPurpose
Raw Force$FI_{raw} = (Close_t - Close_{t-1}) \times Volume_t$Power measurement
Smoothed FI$EFI = EMA_n(FI_{raw})$Noise reduction

Physics Analogy (Newton's 2nd Law):

$$Force = Mass \times Acceleration$$

$$Force_{Index} = Volume \times Price_{Change}$$

Variables:

- $Close_t$ = Today's closing price

- $Close_{t-1}$ = Yesterday's closing price

- $Volume_t$ = Today's trading volume

- $n$ = Smoothing period (typically 2 or 13)

Step-by-Step Logic:

1. Calculate raw force: (Today's Close - Yesterday's Close) × Volume

2. Apply EMA smoothing with period $n$

3. 2-period EMA: Pinpoint entries within established trends

4. 13-period EMA: Identify intermediate trend direction

Real-World Analogy

Think of the Force Index as a punch power meter in boxing. A jab (small price move, low volume) registers low force. A knockout punch (price move + high volume) registers massive force. The Force Index tells you whether the market is throwing jabs or haymakers—and in which direction.

4. Market Context

Best: Trending markets with pullback opportunities

Good: Markets with clear volume patterns

Worst: Choppy/ranging markets (volume erratic, meaningless noise)

Volume Data Requirements:

- Stocks: Actual share volume (ideal)

- Forex: Tick volume (proxy, less reliable)

- Crypto: Exchange-specific (varies by venue)

7. Practical Cautions

Missing Volume = False Signals: If Volume = 0 or NaN (common in Forex feeds or holidays), Force Index drops to 0, potentially triggering false "trend slowing" interpretations.
Volume Data Inconsistency: Different providers measure volume differently. Tick volume (Forex) behaves very differently from share volume (stocks). Recalibrate thresholds per data source.
Never Counter-Trend: The 2-period EFI is for entries WITH the trend, not against it. Don't buy a 2-EMA dip if the 13-EMA is falling.

8. Pro Insights

The "Spike Fade": A massive Force Index spike (3× normal size) often marks:

- Positive Spike: Blow-off top (euphoria → exhaustion)

- Negative Spike: Capitulation bottom (panic → exhaustion)

When EFI spikes to an extreme and immediately collapses back toward zero, "smart money" is using that liquidity to exit. Fade the move.

Volume Confirmation Rule: Never trust a price breakout without Force Index confirmation. If price breaks resistance but EFI stays negative, the breakout lacks conviction and is likely to fail.

Divergence Detection: If price makes a new high but EFI(13) makes a lower high, volume is not supporting the rally. This bearish divergence warns of an impending reversal—often more reliably than price-only divergences.

9. Summary

"The Force Index reveals whether price moves have muscle or are merely empty calories—combining volume with price change to measure true market conviction."

PART 2: SUPPLEMENTARY GUIDE

1. Core Concept

The Force Index answers: "How much conviction is behind this move?"

Dr. Elder argued that price change alone is insufficient:

- A 5% move on low volume = "fluff" (weak)

- A 1% move on massive volume = "concrete" (strong)

By multiplying Price Change × Volume, you get a measure of "power" that distinguishes between meaningful moves and noise.

2. Statistical Edge

The Force Index provides:

- Volume-weighted momentum confirmation

- Early warning of trend exhaustion via divergence

- 2-period version reduces drawdown in pullback entries by 20-30%

- Divergence signals correct more often than RSI divergence in trending markets

3. Actionable Strategy: "Elder's Buy the Dip"


STRATEGY: Force Index Pullback Entry
SETUP: Price > 22-EMA (Uptrend)
       EFI(13) > 0 (Intermediate momentum bullish)
TRIGGER: EFI(2) drops below 0 (Short-term weakness)
ENTRY: Buy stop above high of the candle that made EFI negative
STOP: Low of pullback - 1× ATR
EXIT: EFI(2) reaches new local high OR price hits resistance

4. Risks & Limitations

- Volume dependency: Fails without reliable volume data

- Choppy market noise: EFI erratic in ranging conditions

- Spike misinterpretation: Don't chase spikes—fade them

- Data feed issues: Tick volume ≠ Real volume

Signal Patterns

Force Index Buy Signal

This signal implements Dr. Elder's classic "buy the dip" strategy, combining trend confirmation with short-term weakness. When the 13-period EFI confirms bullish intermediate momentum and price is above the 22-EMA, a negative 2-period EFI indicates a temporary pullback—an ideal entry point. This method reduces drawdown in pullback entries by 20-30% compared to random entries.


def force_index_buy_signal(efi_13, efi_2, price, ema_22):
    uptrend = price > ema_22
    trend_bullish = efi_13 > 0
    pullback = efi_2 < 0
    return uptrend and trend_bullish and pullback

Force Index Divergence

This pattern detects bearish divergence where price makes a higher high but Force Index makes a lower high, indicating volume is not supporting the rally. Divergences in Force Index are often more reliable than price-only divergences because they incorporate volume conviction. This warning signal typically precedes significant price reversals.


def force_index_divergence(price_high, prev_price_high, efi, prev_efi):
    higher_price = price_high > prev_price_high
    lower_efi = efi < prev_efi
    return higher_price and lower_efi  # Bearish divergence
