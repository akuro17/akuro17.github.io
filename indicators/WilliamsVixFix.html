Williams Vix Fix (WVF)

PART 1: MAIN GUIDE

1. Identity

Full Name: Williams Vix Fix

Abbreviation: WVF, VIX Fix

Category: Volatility / Sentiment

Creator: Larry Williams (2007)

One-Sentence Definition: The Williams Vix Fix is a synthetic volatility indicator that replicates VIX behavior for any asset by measuring the percentage deviation of the current Low from the Highest Close, identifying fear spikes that typically mark market bottoms.

2. Calculation Logic

Formula

$$WVF_t = \frac{Highest\ Close_N - Low_t}{Highest\ Close_N} \times 100$$

Where:

- $Highest\ Close_N$ = Highest closing price over the last N periods (default 22)

- $Low_t$ = Current bar's low price

StepLogicPurpose
1Find Highest Close in N periodsReference high point
2Calculate drawdown from highCurrent Low vs. Highest Close
3Normalize as percentageComparable across assets

Output Range: 0 to 100%+

- 0%: Current Low equals recent Highest Close (no drawdown)

- High Values: Significant drop from recent highs (fear/panic)

Real-World Analogy

Think of WVF as a "fear thermometer." When markets are calm and rising, the thermometer reads near zero. When prices crash and panic selling occurs, the thermometer spikes—the bigger the spike, the more extreme the fear.

7. Practical Cautions

Lag Characteristics

ComponentCharacteristic
TypeCoincident (spikes with volatility)
Signal TimingDelayed until spike subsides
Setup vs. Trigger: WVF indicates a SETUP (volatility spike), not an immediate trigger. Wait for the indicator to peak and turn down.
Bear Market Trap: Buying fear spikes in a bear market is "catching a falling knife." Always filter with 200 SMA.

Mitigation Strategies

- Wait for VIX Fix histogram to peak and start declining

- Only take "Fear" buy signals when long-term trend is UP

- Use percentage-based or ATR-based stops, not indicator-based

8. Pro Insights

The "Gray Bar" Signal

Many charting scripts color the VIX Fix histogram—green for high volatility, gray when volatility has peaked and is contracting. The Gray Bar is often the precise moment to enter a mean-reversion long trade.

Percentile Rank Approach

Calculate the 90-day percentile rank of WVF. When the current reading is in the 99th percentile, the statistical odds of a bounce are extremely high (assuming bull market context).

PART 2: SUPPLEMENTARY GUIDE

1. Core Concept

The CBOE VIX requires a liquid options chain—most assets don't have this. Larry Williams' solution was to create a "synthetic VIX" using only price data.

The logic: the VIX spikes when markets crash, and crashes are characterized by rapid deviation from recent highs. By measuring how far the current Low has fallen from the Highest Close, WVF captures the same "fear" signature without options data.

2. Statistical Edge

Why It Works

- Fear (measured by volatility) tends to spike and mean-revert

- Panic selling often overshoots fair value, creating buying opportunities

- High WVF readings correlate with emotional capitulation

3. Actionable Strategy

Strategy Name: "Panic Bottom Catcher"

Entry Rules:

1. Price must be above 200-period SMA (Bull market filter)

2. WVF pierces its own 20-period Upper Bollinger Band (2 StdDev)

3. RSI(14) is below 30

4. Enter when WVF histogram begins declining (peak confirmed)

Exit Rules:

1. Hold for 5-10 days (mean reversion window)

2. OR take profit when RSI > 50

3. Stop loss: Below the low of the spike day

Position Sizing: Aggressive 3% risk (high-probability setup)

Signal Patterns

Panic Bottom Signal

This signal fires when WVF spikes above its upper Bollinger Band coinciding with RSI oversold conditions, indicating extreme fear and potential capitulation. The SMA(200) filter is critical—buying fear spikes in bear markets is "catching a falling knife" with high failure rates. When aligned with an uptrend, this triple-confluence pattern marks high-probability bottom entries with reported 90th percentile success rates.


def vix_fix_buy_signal(wvf, upper_bb, rsi, price, sma200):
    return (
        wvf[-1] > upper_bb[-1] and
        rsi[-1] < 30 and
        price[-1] > sma200[-1]
    )

Peak Detection (Gray Bar)

This pattern confirms the WVF spike has peaked and is now contracting, indicating the worst of the fear has passed. Waiting for the peak rather than entering during the spike significantly improves timing—entering on first spike often means buying into continued selling, while the "gray bar" (histogram turning down) marks the precise inflection point. This peak confirmation transforms a setup into an actionable trigger.


def vix_fix_peak_confirmed(wvf):
    return wvf[-1] < wvf[-2] and wvf[-2] > wvf[-3]  # Turned down
