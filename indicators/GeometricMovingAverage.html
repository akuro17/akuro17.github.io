Geometric Moving Average

PART 1: MAIN GUIDE

1. Identity

Full Name: Geometric Moving Average

Abbreviation: GMA

Category: Trend Following / Smoothing

Creator: Statistical concept applied to finance

One-Sentence Definition: The Geometric Moving Average calculates the n-th root of the product of prices, providing a trend line that dampens outlier spikes and accurately tracks compound growth trajectories.

2. Calculation Logic

Formula

MethodFormulaUse Case
Direct$GMA = \sqrt[n]{\prod_{i=0}^{n-1} P_{t-i}}$Conceptual
Log-Sum$GMA = \exp\left(\frac{1}{n} \sum_{i=0}^{n-1} \ln(P_{t-i})\right)$Implementation

Why Log-Sum? Direct multiplication causes overflow (100^20 is astronomical). Using logarithms keeps numbers manageable.

Mathematical Property:

$$GMA \leq SMA \text{ (always)}$$

This is due to the Arithmetic-Geometric Mean Inequality.

Step-by-Step Logic:

1. Take the natural logarithm of each closing price in the window

2. Calculate the arithmetic mean of these log values

3. Apply exponential function to convert back to price scale

4. Result is the geometric mean

Real-World Analogy

Think of the GMA like calculating average investment returns. If you gained 100% year 1 and lost 50% year 2, your arithmetic average is +25%, but your geometric average (compounded reality) is 0%. The GMA tracks this "true" compounded growth rate of prices, ignoring misleading arithmetic averages.

4. Market Context

Best: Assets with exponential/parabolic growth patterns (crypto, tech stocks)

Good: All trending markets as noise filter

Worst: Mean-reverting, rangebound markets (GMA flattens and lags)

Why It Excels with Growth Stocks:

Standard SMAs get distorted by massive outliers (stock doubles in one day). The geometric root operation inherently dampens these spikes, tracking the "true" trend curve.

7. Practical Cautions

Non-Positive Prices Fatal: If ANY price in the window is zero or negative, the logarithm is undefined and calculation fails. Filter out bad data before calculation.
Overflow Risk: Never calculate product directly ($\prod P_i$). Always use sum of logarithms: $\exp(\frac{1}{n}\sum \ln(P_i))$.
Slight Additional Lag: GMA typically lags slightly more than SMA in sharp spikes but provides cleaner signals in volatile conditions.

8. Pro Insights

The "Crypto/Growth" Smoother: Bitcoin and tech stocks often double or halve in short periods. Standard SMAs become distorted by these outliers for weeks. The GMA naturally dampens the effect of a single extreme candle:

- Use GMA on parabolic assets to find the "true" trendline

- The GMA tracks compound growth more accurately than SMA

Slope as Signal: Rather than waiting for price crossovers, monitor the GMA's derivative (slope). When the GMA stops falling, flattens (slope ≈ 0), and turns up, it's a precise inflection signal that often leads price.

Portfolio Application: GMA is preferred in quantitative finance for calculating geometric mean returns—it's the mathematically correct way to measure compound performance over time.

PART 2: SUPPLEMENTARY GUIDE

1. Core Concept

The GMA answers: "What is the compound growth rate of this asset?"

While SMA uses arithmetic mean (simple average), GMA uses geometric mean (root of products). The difference matters for multiplicative processes like returns:

- 50% gain followed by 50% loss = 0% arithmetic average but -25% geometric reality

- GMA captures this compounding effect in the price trend

2. Statistical Edge

The GMA provides:

- Superior outlier damping (single spikes have minimal impact)

- Accurate representation of compound growth trajectories

- 15-20% fewer false signals in highly volatile assets vs SMA

- Mathematically appropriate for log-scale price analysis

3. Actionable Strategy: "Compounding Acceleration"


STRATEGY: GMA Trend + ROC Confirmation
SETUP: Price above GMA(50) for 10+ bars
TRIGGER: ROC(10) crosses above 0
CONFIRMATION: GMA(50) slope positive
ENTRY: Close of trigger candle
STOP: GMA(50) - 1.5× ATR
EXIT: ROC(10) < 0 for 3 consecutive bars

4. Risks & Limitations

- Zero/negative prices crash: Must filter data quality

- Slight lag vs SMA: In sharp, clean moves

- Overflow without log method: Never multiply directly

- Flat in ranges: No advantage over SMA in sideways markets

Signal Patterns

GMA Buy Signal

This signal confirms an uptrend by requiring price above GMA, a rising GMA, and positive ROC momentum. The triple confirmation filters out false signals in volatile assets where standard SMAs become distorted by outliers. GMA generates 15-20% fewer false signals in highly volatile assets compared to SMA due to its geometric nature.


def gma_buy_signal(price, gma, gma_prev, roc):
    above_gma = price > gma
    gma_rising = gma > gma_prev
    momentum_positive = roc > 0
    return above_gma and gma_rising and momentum_positive

GMA Slope Inflection Signal

This pattern detects the precise moment when GMA transitions from falling to rising—a valley inflection point. Rather than waiting for price crossovers, monitoring the slope derivative provides earlier signals. This inflection often leads price at turning points, making it a high-probability entry trigger in compounding growth trends.


def gma_slope_inflection(gma, gma_prev, gma_prev2):
    was_falling = gma_prev < gma_prev2
    now_rising = gma > gma_prev
    return was_falling and now_rising  # Valley inflection
