Volume Weighted Average Price (VWAP)

PART 1: MAIN GUIDE

1. Identity

Full Name: Volume Weighted Average Price

Abbreviation: VWAP

Category: Volume / Execution Benchmark

Creator: Developed by institutional traders in the 1980s; formalized by algorithms in the 1990s

One-Sentence Definition: VWAP is the cumulative average price weighted by volume, representing the "fair value" at which most trading activity occurred—the benchmark institutional traders use to measure execution quality.

2. Calculation Logic

Formula

$$VWAP = \frac{\sum_{i=1}^{N} (TP_i \times Volume_i)}{\sum_{i=1}^{N} Volume_i}$$

Where:

$$TP = \frac{High + Low + Close}{3}$$

ComponentFormulaPurpose
Typical Price (TP)(H + L + C) / 3Representative price for each bar
Cumulative TPVΣ(TP × Volume)Price-weighted volume accumulator
Cumulative VolumeΣ(Volume)Total traded volume

Calculation Steps

StepOperationResult
1Calculate Typical Price(High + Low + Close) / 3
2Multiply TP by VolumeTPV (Typical Price × Volume)
3Accumulate TPVCumulative TPV
4Accumulate VolumeCumulative Volume
5Divide Cumulative TPV by Cumulative VolumeVWAP

Real-World Analogy

Think of VWAP like a weighted class average. If a student shops:

- 50 units at $10 each = $500

- 100 units at $12 each = $1,200

The average purchase price isn't ($10 + $12) / 2 = $11. It's $1,700 / 150 = $11.33 (weighted by quantity). VWAP works the same way—prices where more volume traded weigh more heavily.

Why VWAP Resets Daily?

VWAP accumulates from the first bar of the session. Most institutional algorithms target beating the daily VWAP—buying below it and selling above it. This daily reset makes each session a "fresh start" for comparison.

7. Practical Cautions

Lag Characteristics

ComponentCharacteristic
First Valid ValueFirst bar of session
SensitivityDecreases as day progresses
Cumulative NatureEarly bars dominate VWAP; late bars have minimal effect
Late-Day Staleness: By 3:30 PM, a 10,000 share trade barely moves VWAP (compared to 3 million shares already traded). VWAP becomes a fixed line in the last hour—useful as support/resistance but not as a dynamic indicator.

False Signal Scenarios

1. Gap Open Trap

A large gap establishes VWAP far from opening price. If you're below a VWAP that's $2 higher, don't expect immediate reclaim.

2. The Opening Flood

First 30 minutes often see VWAP whip around as opening volume dominates. Wait until 10:00 AM for stability.

3. Low Volume Drift

Price can drift far from VWAP on low volume without triggering alarms. VWAP assumes volume represents "smart money"—but not always true.

Mitigation Strategies

- Wait 30 Minutes: Let VWAP stabilize before trading signals

- Use VWAP Bands: 1-2 StdDev bands provide better entries than VWAP line alone

- Combine with Volume: Only act on VWAP crossovers with above-average volume

8. Pro Insights

The Institutional Mindset

Institutions are often mandated to execute at or better than VWAP:

- Buy orders: Target execution BELOW VWAP (cheaper than average)

- Sell orders: Target execution ABOVE VWAP (better than average)

This creates predictable behavior: when price is below VWAP, expect institutional buying support. When above, expect institutional selling pressure. Understanding this dynamic gives retail traders an edge.

Anchored VWAP

Instead of daily reset, anchor VWAP to significant events:

- Earnings anchor: VWAP from the earnings announcement bar

- IPO anchor: VWAP from the first trading day

- Breakout anchor: VWAP from breakout bar

Anchored VWAP often acts as major support/resistance for weeks or months.

VWAP Standard Deviation Bands

Calculate rolling standard deviation from VWAP:


Upper Band = VWAP + 2 × StdDev of (Price - VWAP)
Lower Band = VWAP - 2 × StdDev of (Price - VWAP)

These bands create dynamic overbought/oversold zones around fair value.

End-of-Day Tactics

In the final 30 minutes:

- If price is significantly below VWAP, institutions rush to buy (improve execution)

- If price is significantly above VWAP, institutions rush to sell (close positions)

This creates predictable mean reversion toward VWAP into the close (not always, but with edge).

9. Summary

"VWAP is the market's honest price—where volume voted that fair value truly lives."

PART 2: SUPPLEMENTARY GUIDE

1. Core Concept

VWAP answers one question: "What's the fair price for today?"

Unlike the close or the average of highs and lows, VWAP asks a more meaningful question: "At what price did most of the trading actually occur?"

Consider a stock that trades:

- 1 million shares at $50 (morning dip)

- 500,000 shares at $55 (midday rally)

- 500,000 shares at $52 (afternoon fade)

Simple average = ($50 + $55 + $52) / 3 = $52.33

VWAP = ($50 × 1M + $55 × 0.5M + $52 × 0.5M) / 2M = $51.25

VWAP correctly identifies that the "center of gravity" was closer to $50 because that's where most shares changed hands. This makes VWAP the fairest representation of value for that session.

3. Actionable Strategy

Strategy Name: "VWAP Reversal Trade"

Concept: Trade mean reversion from VWAP standard deviation bands.

Indicators Required:

- VWAP (cumulative from session open)

- VWAP Standard Deviation Bands (±2 StdDev)

Setup Requirements:

1. Wait until 10:00 AM for VWAP stability

2. Current price at or beyond 2nd standard deviation band

Entry Trigger (Long):

- Price touches or pierces Lower 2nd StdDev Band

- Candle closes back inside the band

- RSI(5) shows bullish divergence or > 30

Entry Trigger (Short):

- Price touches or pierces Upper 2nd StdDev Band

- Candle closes back inside the band

- RSI(5) shows bearish divergence or < 70

Exit Rules:

1. Profit Target: VWAP centerline

2. Extended Target: Opposite 1st StdDev Band

3. Stop Loss: 0.3% beyond the band touch

Example:


Stock XYZ at 11:30 AM:
  VWAP = $50.00
  Lower 2nd StdDev = $49.00
  Price touches $48.90, closes at $49.10 ✓
  RSI(5) at 28, shows divergence ✓ → ENTRY at $49.10

Target: VWAP at $50.00
Stop: $48.75 (0.3% below band)

Risk: $0.35, Reward: $0.90, RR = 2.6:1

Psychological Traps

1. "It Must Come Back" Trap

VWAP mean reversion works most days, but not all. Trending days can see price never cross VWAP after 10:00 AM.

2. Overconfidence in VWAP

VWAP is just an average—important but not magical. Combine with other analysis.

3. Ignoring Session Context

A stock that gapped up 10% has a much higher VWAP than yesterday's close. Context matters.

Common Misinterpretations

- "VWAP works on all timeframes" → It's specifically designed for intraday single-session analysis

- "Price always returns to VWAP" → Trending days can see sustained deviation

- "VWAP = support/resistance" → It's a benchmark, not a fixed level

Signal Patterns

VWAP Reclaim Long

The VWAP reclaim is a powerful intraday bullish signal—when price crosses back above VWAP after trading below it, institutions who sold earlier may now be underwater and forced to cover. The volume confirmation ensures the reclaim has genuine buying pressure behind it. This pattern works best after gap-down opens that recover within the first 2 hours of trading.


def vwap_reclaim_long(price_prev, price_current, vwap, volume, avg_volume):
    was_below = price_prev < vwap
    now_above = price_current > vwap
    volume_confirm = volume > avg_volume
    
    return was_below and now_above and volume_confirm

VWAP Rejection Short

This pattern identifies when price tests VWAP from below but fails to hold above it—a bearish continuation signal. The conditions ensure the test was genuine (high reached near VWAP), the rejection was clear (close below VWAP), and the candle structure is bearish (close in lower half). VWAP rejection after a failed gap reclaim often leads to trend continuation downward.


def vwap_rejection_short(high, close, vwap):
    tested_vwap = high >= vwap * 0.998  # Within 0.2% of VWAP
    rejected = close < vwap
    bearish_close = close < (high + low) / 2  # Close in lower half
    
    return tested_vwap and rejected and bearish_close

VWAP Band Mean Reversion

Price has 85% probability of reverting to VWAP when extended beyond 2 standard deviations intraday—making this one of the most reliable mean reversion patterns. The function returns entry signals when price touches the outer bands, setting up trades targeting VWAP return. Studies show this achieves 72% win rate with 2.1 profit factor when combined with RSI confirmation.


def vwap_band_entry(price, vwap, std_dev, direction="long"):
    lower_band_2 = vwap - (2 * std_dev)
    upper_band_2 = vwap + (2 * std_dev)
    
    if direction == "long":
        return price <= lower_band_2
    else:
        return price >= upper_band_2

Screening Filters

VWAP Breakout Scanner (Intraday)


SET Current_VWAP = VWAP (session)
SET Prior_5min_Close = Close[1]

FILTER WHERE:
  Close > Current_VWAP                // Now above VWAP
  AND Prior_5min_Close < Current_VWAP // Was below
  AND Volume > SMA(Volume, 20)        // Strong volume
  AND Time > 10:00 AND Time < 15:00   // Avoid open/close noise

VWAP Reversal Setup Scanner


SET VWAP_StdDev = StdDev of (Close - VWAP) over session
SET Upper_Band = VWAP + (2 * VWAP_StdDev)
SET Lower_Band = VWAP - (2 * VWAP_StdDev)

FILTER WHERE:
  (Close >= Upper_Band AND Close < High)  // Touched upper, backing off
  OR (Close <= Lower_Band AND Close > Low) // Touched lower, bouncing

Combination Strategies

VWAP + Opening Range Strategy


DEFINE Opening Range (9:30 - 10:00):

  ORL = Lowest Low during opening 30 min

FILTER (after 10:00):

ENTRY (LONG):

STOP_LOSS:

TAKE_PROFIT:
