Weighted Moving Average (WMA)

PART 1: MAIN GUIDE

1. Identity

Full Name: Weighted Moving Average

Abbreviation: WMA

Category: Trend / Overlay

One-Sentence Definition: The Weighted Moving Average is a trend-following indicator that assigns linearly decreasing weights to price data, placing greater emphasis on recent prices to reduce lag compared to a Simple Moving Average.

2. Calculation Logic

Formula

$$WMA_t = \frac{\sum_{i=0}^{N-1} (Price_{t-i} \times (N-i))}{\frac{N(N+1)}{2}}$$

Where:

- $N$ = Period (lookback window)

- Weight for most recent price = $N$

- Weight for oldest price = $1$

- Denominator = Sum of weights (Triangular Number)

StepLogicPurpose
1Calculate denominator = N×(N+1)/2Sum of all weights
2Multiply each price by its weightRecent = N, Oldest = 1
3Sum weighted pricesWeighted numerator
4Divide by denominatorFinal WMA value

Real-World Analogy

Imagine grading a student where the most recent test counts 5x, the previous 4x, and so on. Recent performance matters more, but past history still contributes—just like WMA prioritizes recent prices while not ignoring older data.

- Do not trade solely on price crossing the WMA

- Use dual WMA system (Fast/Slow) for confirmation

8. Pro Insights

The "Slope" Signal

Professional traders focus on the slope of the WMA more than price crosses. If price dips below the WMA but the WMA line is still pointing up, this is often a buying opportunity (dip) rather than a reversal.

Dynamic Support/Resistance

In strong trends, the WMA acts as a "magnetic" support/resistance level where pullbacks often find buyers or sellers.

PART 2: SUPPLEMENTARY GUIDE

1. Core Concept

The WMA addresses a fundamental flaw of the Simple Moving Average: treating all data points equally. In markets, what happened yesterday is far more relevant to the current state than what happened two weeks ago.

By assigning linearly decreasing weights (5, 4, 3, 2, 1 for a 5-period WMA), the WMA shifts the "center of gravity" of the filter toward the present.

2. Statistical Edge

Why It Works

- Studies show recent price action has higher autocorrelation with future prices

- WMA's lag reduction of ~25-30% compared to SMA allows earlier trend detection

- Earlier entries capture additional profit in trending moves

3. Actionable Strategy

Strategy Name: "WMA Pullback Entry"

Entry Rules:

1. Price must be above 50-period WMA (Trend confirmation)

2. Wait for CCI(20) to dip below -100 (Oversold pullback)

3. Enter when CCI crosses back above -100

4. WMA slope must be positive

Exit Rules:

1. Price closes below WMA(50)

2. OR CCI > +100 (Overbought)

Position Sizing: 1.5% risk per trade

Signal Patterns

WMA Slope Turn Signal

This signal fires when the WMA slope transitions from negative to positive, indicating a momentum shift from bearish to bullish. Professional traders emphasize slope direction over price crosses because the WMA's linear weighting makes slope changes more meaningful than temporary price fluctuations. The slope turn often precedes price action by 1-2 bars, providing early entry opportunities.


def wma_slope_turn_bullish(wma):
    current_slope = wma[-1] - wma[-2]
    previous_slope = wma[-2] - wma[-3]
    return previous_slope < 0 and current_slope > 0

Dual WMA Crossover

The Golden Cross occurs when the fast WMA (typically 20-period) crosses above the slow WMA (50-period), signaling the emergence of short-term momentum overtaking the longer trend. This dual-confirmation approach reduces whipsaws compared to single-line methods because both timeframes must align. Studies show lag reduction of approximately 25-30% versus SMA crossovers.


def wma_golden_cross(fast_wma, slow_wma):
    return (
        fast_wma[-1] > slow_wma[-1] and
        fast_wma[-2] <= slow_wma[-2]
    )
