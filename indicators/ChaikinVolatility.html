ChaikinVolatility (CV)

PART 1: MAIN GUIDE

1. Identity

Full Name: Chaikin Volatility

Abbreviation: CV

Category: Volatility

Creator: Marc Chaikin (1980s)

One-Sentence Definition: Chaikin Volatility measures the rate of change of the average trading range—positive values indicate volatility is expanding (market getting excited), negative values indicate volatility is contracting (market getting bored).

2. Calculation Logic

Formula

$$CV = \frac{EMA(H-L)_t - EMA(H-L)_{t-n}}{EMA(H-L)_{t-n}} \times 100$$

StepFormulaPurpose
1$Range = High - Low$Daily trading range
2$EMA_{HL} = EMA(Range, n)$Smoothed average range (usually n=10)
3$ROC = \frac{EMA_{HL,t} - EMA_{HL,t-n}}{EMA_{HL,t-n}}$Rate of change of smoothed range
4$CV = ROC \times 100$Percentage format

Key Insight

Chaikin Volatility is Rate of Change applied to the Exponential Moving Average of the High-Low range. It doesn't measure absolute volatility—it measures how fast volatility is changing.

Real-World Analogy

Think of CV as a volatility speedometer. ATR tells you how wide the road is (current volatility). CV tells you if the road is widening (positive, increasing volatility) or narrowing (negative, decreasing volatility). A rising CV means the market is waking up; a falling CV means it's going to sleep.

7. Practical Cautions

False Signal Scenarios

1. Direction Confusion

CV rising doesn't mean price is rising—it only means volatility is expanding. Price could crash with rising CV.

2. Division by Zero

If the EMA of range is 0 (flatline price), the formula breaks.

3. Intraday Misapplication

CV is designed for daily ranges. Using it on 5-minute charts produces misleading signals.

Mitigation Strategies

- ALWAYS pair with a directional indicator (MA, price breakout, trend filter)

- Handle edge cases — return null or 0 when EMA of range is 0

- Look for "base building" — CV negative for extended period before spike

- Don't trade CV alone — it's a confirmation tool, not a signal generator

8. Pro Insights

The Volatility Crush Entry

In a bull market, the best entries come when:

1. CV has been negative for an extended period (volatility compressed)

2. Price is consolidating near support or a moving average

3. CV starts rising AND price breaks short-term resistance

This "volatility crush" setup often precedes the largest moves of the year.

Top Spotting with CV

A slow, grinding uptrend with declining CV often indicates a healthy bull market—steady, orderly accumulation.

A sudden spike in CV at a new price high often marks a "blow-off top"—the final surge of FOMO before reversal.

Bottom Spotting with CV

Panic selling produces CV spikes (everyone rushing for the exits). When CV turns down from a spike during a crash, it signals:

- Panic is subsiding

- Sellers are exhausted

- Safe to look for reversal trades

Never Trade CV Alone

CV has zero directional information. A rising CV only tells you the range is expanding—it could be expanding up OR down. Always combine with:

- Price breakout direction

- Moving average position

- Prior trend context

9. Summary

"Chaikin Volatility tells you if the market is waking up (rising CV = expanding range) or falling asleep (falling CV = contracting range). Use volatility compression as a launchpad for breakouts, and watch for climax spikes to spot reversals—but always pair with a directional indicator."

PART 2: SUPPLEMENTARY GUIDE

1. Core Concept

Chaikin Volatility answers: "Is the trading range expanding or contracting compared to 10 days ago?"

- CV > 0: Today's average range is larger than 10 days ago → Volatility expanding

- CV < 0: Today's average range is smaller than 10 days ago → Volatility contracting

- CV = 0: Range unchanged

The EMA smoothing prevents single-day outliers from distorting the signal.

CV is fundamentally different from ATR:

- ATR = absolute volatility level

- CV = rate of change of volatility

2. Statistical Edge

Volatility Crush Performance

- Average move size: 1.8x larger than normal breakouts

Signal Patterns

Volatility Expansion Detection

The transition from negative to positive CV signals that volatility is expanding after a compression phase. This often coincides with breakouts and the beginning of significant price moves. The signal is directionless—you must use price breakout direction to determine whether to go long or short.

Volatility Climax Detection

Extreme CV spikes (95th percentile or higher) followed by a decline indicate volatility climaxes. In downtrends, this signals panic capitulation and potential bottoms; in uptrends, it signals euphoria and potential tops. The reversal is more reliable when CV has already started declining from the spike.

Extended Coil Pattern

When CV remains negative for 20+ consecutive bars, the market is building potential energy through volatility compression. This "coil" pattern often precedes explosive moves, with subsequent breakouts averaging 1.8x the size of normal breakouts.

Breakout Confirmation

CV provides crucial validation for price breakouts. A breakout accompanied by positive CV (expanding volatility) has genuine energy behind it. Breakouts with negative CV are suspect and more likely to fail, as the market lacks the momentum to sustain the move.


def cv_expansion(cv_values):
    """Detect volatility expansion from compression"""
    crossed_positive = cv_values[-2] < 0 and cv_values[-1] > 0
    return crossed_positive

def cv_climax(cv_values, percentile_threshold=95, lookback=120):
    """Detect volatility climax (spike)"""
    import numpy as np
    threshold = np.percentile([v for v in cv_values[-lookback:] if v is not None], percentile_threshold)
    is_spike = cv_values[-1] > threshold
    is_turning = cv_values[-1] < cv_values[-2]  # Starting to decline
    return is_spike and is_turning

def cv_coil(cv_values, min_bars=20):
    """Detect extended volatility compression (coil)"""
    negative_count = sum(1 for v in cv_values[-min_bars:] if v is not None and v < 0)
    return negative_count >= min_bars

def cv_breakout_confirmation(cv_value, price, prev_high, prev_low):
    """Confirm breakout with CV expansion"""
    price_breakout = price > prev_high or price < prev_low
    cv_expanding = cv_value > 0
    return price_breakout and cv_expanding
