Double Exponential Moving Average

PART 1: MAIN GUIDE

1. Identity

Full Name: Double Exponential Moving Average

Abbreviation: DEMA

Category: Trend Following / Lag Reduction

Creator: Patrick Mulloy (1994)

One-Sentence Definition: DEMA is a lag-reduced moving average using the formula 2×EMA - EMA(EMA) to cancel out inherent lag, producing a trend line that hugs price closely.

2. Calculation Logic

Formula

$$DEMA = 2 \times EMA_n(Price) - EMA_n(EMA_n(Price))$$

StepFormulaPurpose
1$EMA_1 = EMA_n(Close)$First-level smoothing
2$EMA_2 = EMA_n(EMA_1)$Second-level smoothing
3$DEMA = 2 \times EMA_1 - EMA_2$Lag cancellation

Real-World Analogy

Think of DEMA like a GPS that predicts your position. Standard GPS shows where you were a moment ago. DEMA calculates velocity and projects where you actually are now.

Overshoot Warning: DEMA can extend beyond price during spikes due to its derivative component.

8. Pro Insights

Velocity Gap: Plot DEMA and EMA together. The gap represents market velocity—wide gap = strong momentum.

9. Summary

"DEMA shows you where the trend IS, not where it WAS, by mathematically canceling out lag."

PART 3: ADVANCED SIGNAL REFERENCE

Signal Patterns

DEMA Cross Buy

This signal triggers when price crosses above the DEMA line while the DEMA itself is rising, confirming bullish momentum. The combination of price breakout and trend acceleration provides a higher-probability entry compared to simple crossovers. Works best in volatile, trending markets where DEMA's reduced lag offers early detection.


def dema_cross_buy(close, dema, close_prev, dema_prev):
    return close > dema and close_prev <= dema_prev and dema > dema_prev

Screening Filters


FILTER = (DEMA_Fast CROSSES ABOVE DEMA_Slow) AND (Volume > SMA(Volume,20)*1.5)
