Wilder's Smoothing (Wilder's Moving Average)

PART 1: MAIN GUIDE

1. Identity

Full Name: Wilder's Smoothing / Wilder's Moving Average

Abbreviation: WilderMA, SMMA

Category: Trend / Smoothing

Creator: J. Welles Wilder Jr. (1978)

One-Sentence Definition: Wilder's Smoothing is a specific exponential moving average method using α = 1/N, making it significantly slower and more stable than standard EMAs.

2. Calculation Logic

Formula

The Wilder's Smoothing is calculated recursively:

$$WilderMA_t = \frac{WilderMA_{t-1} \times (N-1) + Price_t}{N}$$

Expanded Form (revealing EMA relationship):

$$WilderMA_t = \left(1 - \frac{1}{N}\right) \times WilderMA_{t-1} + \frac{1}{N} \times Price_t$$

Smoothing TypeAlpha (α)14-Period Equivalent
Wilder's MA1/N = 1/14 ≈ 0.071Equivalent to 27-period EMA
Standard EMA2/(N+1) = 2/15 ≈ 0.133Standard 14-period EMA

Real-World Analogy

Think of Wilder's Smoothing like a heavy freight train. Once it starts moving in a direction, it takes significant force to change course. This inertia makes it excellent for identifying major trend shifts but slow to react to minor fluctuations.

7. Practical Cautions

Lag Characteristics

ComponentCharacteristic
Alpha1/N (very slow)
Equivalent LagWilder 14 ≈ EMA 27
Initialization Bias: WilderMA is seeded with an SMA. Early values differ depending on seed method, causing backtest discrepancies.
NaN Propagation: Recursive calculation means a single bad data point corrupts all future values.

Mitigation Strategies

- Use WilderMA as a regime filter, not a trading signal

- Pair with faster EMA for triggering entries

- Ensure sufficient historical data (3-4x period) for convergence

8. Pro Insights

The Baseline Trick

Use WilderMA(50) as a "Bull/Bear Territory" line. When price is above it, only look for buy signals on faster oscillators. This dramatically improves win rates by filtering counter-trend trades.

Institutional Use

Many fund managers use WilderMA(200) as the definitive long-term trend indicator for asset allocation decisions due to its resistance to whipsaws.

9. Summary

"Wilder's Smoothing is the anchor that keeps your trading ship from drifting with every market ripple."

PART 2: SUPPLEMENTARY GUIDE

1. Core Concept

Wilder designed his smoothing method in the 1970s when calculations were done by hand. He needed a recursive formula that could be updated daily without storing the entire price history.

The key innovation is using α = 1/N instead of the more common α = 2/(N+1). This creates a significantly slower-moving average that retains "memory" of historical data much longer.

2. Statistical Edge

Why It Works

- Extended memory depth filters out noise that traps traders using faster indicators

- Confirmed crosses are statistically significant—they represent genuine regime changes

3. Actionable Strategy

Strategy Name: "Momentum Ribbon Strategy"

Setup:

- Plot 14-period WilderMA (Slow anchor)

- Plot 14-period Standard EMA (Fast trigger)

Entry Rules:

1. Standard EMA crosses above WilderMA (Ribbon expands)

2. Gap between EMA and WilderMA is widening

3. Price is above both lines

Exit Rules:

1. Standard EMA collapses back toward WilderMA

2. OR EMA crosses below WilderMA

Position Sizing: Conservative 1% risk

Signal Patterns

Momentum Ribbon Expansion

This pattern triggers when the faster EMA stays above the slower WilderMA with a widening spread, indicating accelerating momentum in the trend direction. The expansion shows that short-term price action is outpacing the slow-moving anchor, confirming genuine trend strength rather than noise. This "ribbon" approach dramatically improves win rates by filtering counter-trend trades.


def ribbon_expansion(ema, wilder_ma):
    current_spread = ema[-1] - wilder_ma[-1]
    previous_spread = ema[-2] - wilder_ma[-2]
    return ema[-1] > wilder_ma[-1] and current_spread > previous_spread

Regime Filter

This simple but powerful filter establishes whether the market is in "bull territory" (price above WilderMA) or "bear territory" (price below). Due to WilderMA's exceptionally slow response (equivalent to a 27-period EMA when using 14-period Wilder), confirmed crosses represent statistically significant regime changes. Many fund managers use WilderMA(200) as the definitive long-term trend indicator for asset allocation decisions.


def bullish_regime(price, wilder_ma):
    return price[-1] > wilder_ma[-1]
