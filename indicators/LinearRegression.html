Linear Regression

PART 1: MAIN GUIDE

1. Identity

Full Name: Linear Regression (Curve/Channel)

Abbreviation: LR / LinReg

Category: Statistical Overlay

Creator: Statistical derivation (Gauss/Legendre)

One-Sentence Definition: The Linear Regression indicator plots the "fair value" of price based on the best-fit line, often with standard deviation channels to identify statistically overextended conditions.

2. Calculation Logic

Formula

The indicator combines the Linear Regression Curve (endpoint) with Standard Deviation channels:

ComponentFormulaPurpose
Slope$m = \frac{N \sum(xy) - \sum x \sum y}{N \sum(x^2) - (\sum x)^2}$Trend direction
Intercept$b = \frac{\sum y - m \sum x}{N}$Y-axis offset
LR Value$y = b + m \times (N-1)$Regression endpoint
R-Squared$R^2 = 1 - \frac{SS_{res}}{SS_{tot}}$Goodness of fit

Channel Construction

$$Upper\ Channel = LR\ Value + (k \times \sigma)$$

$$Lower\ Channel = LR\ Value - (k \times \sigma)$$

Where $\sigma$ is standard deviation of residuals and $k$ is typically 2.0 (95% confidence).

Real-World Analogy

Think of Linear Regression like the flight path of an airplane. The regression line is the intended trajectory—where the plane should be. The channels are the acceptable deviation boundaries. If the plane (price) drifts to the edge of the boundary, either the pilot (market) corrects back to the path (mean reversion) or something has fundamentally changed (breakout).

7. Practical Cautions

False Signal Scenarios

1. Band Walking

In strong trends, price can ride the 2SD band—don't fade mechanically.

2. Repainting Charts

Some platforms fit lines to all data including future—verify your tool plots *endpoint* only.

Mitigation Strategies

- Close Confirmation: Require candle close back inside channel

- Pearson R Filter: If correlation < 0.40, channel is unreliable

- Don't Fade Trends: Check ADX; if > 30, don't mean-revert

8. Pro Insights

The Raff Channel Technique

Standard channels use SD. A "Raff Channel" sets width based on the *furthest* price point from the regression line—containing 100% of data. A break of a Raff Channel is significant because price is doing something unprecedented in the lookback period.

2SD = 95% Confidence

Statistically, 95% of data should fall within 2 SDs. A touch of the 2SD line means price is in the top/bottom 2.5% of expected values—a statistical anomaly suggesting mean reversion.

9. Summary

"Linear Regression maps price's statistical fair value—the center is equilibrium, and the channels mark where price has mathematically overreached."

PART 2: SUPPLEMENTARY GUIDE

1. Core Concept

Linear Regression with channels answers: "What's the statistically normal range for price, and when has it deviated abnormally?"

The center line is the "expected" price if current trajectory continues. The channels define the normal distribution boundaries around that trajectory.

2. Statistical Edge

The 2SD channel represents a 95% confidence interval. Price touching this boundary is a 1-in-20 event—statistically significant. Mean reversion strategies exploit this by betting that price returns to the mean.

3. Actionable Strategy

Strategy Name: "Channel Extremes Fade"

Setup: Price at or beyond 2SD channel

Entry: Close back inside channel + CCI confirming

Stop: 1 ATR beyond channel edge

Target: Center Line (regression value)

4. Risks & Limitations

- Trend Persistence: Strong trends ignore mean reversion

- Zero Variance: Flat markets break the calculation

- Repainting: Ensure tool doesn't use future data

PART 3: ADVANCED SIGNAL REFERENCE

Signal Patterns

Linear Regression Channel Buy Signal

This signal identifies mean reversion opportunities when price touches or pierces the Lower 2SD channel. Statistically, only 2.5% of data should fall below 2 standard deviations—making this a statistically significant oversold condition. The R² filter ensures the regression channel is meaningful, not fitted to random scatter.


def lr_channel_buy(price, lower_2sd, lr_value, r_squared):
    at_lower = price <= lower_2sd
    valid_fit = r_squared > 0.30
    return at_lower and valid_fit

Screening Filters


Price <= Lower_LR_Channel(50, 2.0)
AND R-Squared(50) > 0.30
AND CCI(14) < -100
