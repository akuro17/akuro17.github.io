ADXR (Average Directional Movement Index Rating)

PART 1: MAIN GUIDE

1. Identity

Full Name: Average Directional Movement Index Rating

Abbreviation: ADXR

Category: Trend Strength

Creator: J. Welles Wilder Jr. (1978)

One-Sentence Definition: ADXR is the smoothed "rating" of trend momentum that measures how sustainable the current trend strength is compared to its recent history.

2. Calculation Logic

Formula

$$ADXR = \frac{ADX_{today} + ADX_{n\ periods\ ago}}{2}$$

Where ADX itself is derived from the Directional Movement System:

StepFormulaPurpose
1$+DM = High_{t} - High_{t-1}$ (if positive and > -DM, else 0)Positive directional movement
2$-DM = Low_{t-1} - Low_{t}$ (if positive and > +DM, else 0)Negative directional movement
3$TR = \max(H-L,H-C_{prev},L-C_{prev})$True Range
4$+DI = \frac{Smoothed(+DM)}{Smoothed(TR)} \times 100$Positive Directional Indicator
5$-DI = \frac{Smoothed(-DM)}{Smoothed(TR)} \times 100$Negative Directional Indicator
6$DX = \frac{+DI - (-DI)}{+DI + (-DI)} \times 100$Directional Index
7$ADX = Wilder\ Smoothing(DX, n)$Average Directional Index
8$ADXR = \frac{ADX_t + ADX_{t-n}}{2}$Final Rating

Real-World Analogy

Think of ADXR like the cruise control setting on a car. While ADX tells you how fast the car is currently going (speedometer), ADXR tells you whether that speed is sustainable over a longer journey. If you're driving 60 mph but your cruise control is set to 40 mph, you're likely to slow down soon—just as a high ADX with falling ADXR signals an unsustainable trend.

Why These Values?

The 14-period setting corresponds to approximately two trading weeks, capturing enough data to smooth out daily noise while remaining responsive to genuine trend changes. The 25 threshold represents the statistical boundary where directional movement becomes significant enough to justify trend-following strategies.

7. Practical Cautions

Lag Characteristics

ComponentApproximate Lag
First Valid ADX~28 bars (2×Period)
First Valid ADXR~42 bars (3×Period)
Signal Responsiveness5-10 bars after trend begins
Reality Check: By the time ADXR confirms a trend, you've likely missed the first 10-15% of the move. Use ADXR for position sizing and exits, not aggressive entries.

False Signal Scenarios

1. The 20-25 No Man's Land

When ADXR hovers between 20-25, signals flip frequently. Wait for decisive break above 25.

2. Falling ADXR Traps

A +DI/-DI crossover is unreliable when ADXR is falling—the trend rating is degrading.

3. High ADXR ≠ More Trend

When ADXR > 50 and hooks down, the trend is exhausting, not strengthening.

Mitigation Strategies

- Use the Slope Filter: Only trade when ADXR slope is positive

- Wait for Pullbacks: Use RSI or Stochastics to enter *within* the confirmed trend

- Respect the 25 Threshold: Treat it as a hard filter, not a suggestion

8. Pro Insights

The ADX/ADXR Crossover Secret

Most traders only watch for ADX > 25. But the ADX crossing above ADXR is the "insider's signal"—it mathematically proves that current momentum exceeds the recent average. This crossover often precedes the most explosive portion of trends by 2-3 bars.

The Early Exit Edge

When ADX crosses *below* ADXR, exit immediately. This "Dead Cross" is often the earliest reliable exit signal, triggering before price shows any weakness. Professional trend followers use this to lock in profits before the crowd realizes the trend is dying.

Portfolio Selection Technique

Screen for stocks where:

- ADXR recently crossed above 25 (past 5 days)

- ADX > ADXR (momentum accelerating)

- Sector's aggregate ADXR also rising

This identifies individual stocks participating in a broader sector rotation.

Sector Rotation Application

Track the ADXR of sector ETFs (XLK, XLF, XLE, etc.). When a sector's ADXR rises from below 20 to above 25, institutional money is flowing in. Rotate capital to the sector with the strongest ADXR reading.

9. Summary

"ADXR is your trend's durability rating—high values confirm you're on a sustainable train, low values warn you're riding a wave that's about to crash."

PART 2: SUPPLEMENTARY GUIDE

1. Core Concept

ADXR answers one question: "Is this trend going to last?"

While ADX tells you how strong the trend is *right now*, ADXR compares today's strength to where it was N periods ago. If ADX is high but ADXR is higher, the trend is actually *weakening*—today's momentum doesn't match yesterday's. If ADX exceeds ADXR, the trend is *accelerating*.

Think of it this way:

- ADX = How fast is the car going right now?

- ADXR = Is the car speeding up or slowing down?

The calculation is elegant in its simplicity: take today's ADX, add the ADX from 14 days ago, divide by 2. This simple average creates a "rating" that smooths out short-term fluctuations and reveals the underlying trend trajectory.

Why It Works

The statistical edge comes from regime identification. Markets spend roughly 30% of time trending and 70% ranging. By only trading when ADXR > 25, you focus on the 30% where trend-following strategies have positive expectancy while avoiding the 70% where they lose money.

Academic Reference

Wilder's Directional Movement System, including ADXR, was validated in his 1978 book *New Concepts in Technical Trading Systems*. Subsequent research by Perry Kaufman and others confirmed that trend-strength filters significantly improve risk-adjusted returns.

Psychological Traps

1. The "It's Still High" Trap

ADXR at 45 feels bullish, but if it was 55 last week and falling, the trend is dying. Always check the *slope*, not just the level.

2. Overconfidence in Strong Readings

ADXR > 50 suggests exhaustion, not strength. Don't add to positions at extreme readings.

3. Impatience During Setup

Waiting for ADXR > 25 can feel like missing trades. Remember: non-trending markets destroy trend systems.

Common Misinterpretations

- "High ADXR means buy" → It measures strength, not direction

- "ADXR confirms price direction" → It's non-directional; check +DI/-DI

- "Cross above 25 is the entry" → It's a filter, not a trigger

Signal Patterns

Buy Signal (Trend Acceleration)

When ADXR exceeds 25 and ADX simultaneously exceeds ADXR with a positive slope, this indicates an accelerating uptrend—momentum is not just present but increasing. Combined with +DI dominating -DI, this setup identifies the "sweet spot" for trend-following entries where the trend is both established and gaining strength.


def adxr_buy_signal(adx, adxr, plus_di, minus_di):
    condition_1 = adxr > 25                    # Trend exists
    condition_2 = adx > adxr                   # Momentum accelerating
    condition_3 = plus_di > minus_di           # Uptrend confirmed
    condition_4 = adxr > adxr_previous         # ADXR slope positive
    
    return all([condition_1, condition_2, condition_3, condition_4])

Sell/Exit Signal (Trend Deceleration)

The "Dead Cross" occurs when ADX falls below ADXR—indicating current momentum has dropped below its recent average, signaling trend fatigue. Alternatively, a direction flip (-DI crossing above +DI in a strong trend) or exhaustion pattern (ADXR above 50 and declining) both warn that the trend phase is ending and positions should be reduced or closed.


def adxr_exit_signal(adx, adxr, plus_di, minus_di):
    dead_cross = adx < adxr and adx_previous >= adxr_previous
    direction_flip = minus_di > plus_di and adxr > 25
    exhaustion = adxr > 50 and adxr < adxr_previous
    
    return dead_cross or direction_flip or exhaustion

Alternative Signal (Trend Birth)

When ADXR rises from below 20 (extreme dormancy), it signals the birth of a new trend from a previously quiet market. Combined with clear directional bias (+DI > -DI for uptrends), this early-stage signal captures the beginning of trends before they become obvious to most traders.


def adxr_trend_birth(adxr, plus_di, minus_di):
    dormant_turning = adxr < 20 and adxr > adxr_previous
    direction_clear = plus_di > minus_di
    
    return dormant_turning and direction_clear
