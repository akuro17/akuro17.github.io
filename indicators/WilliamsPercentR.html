Williams %R (Williams Percent Range)

PART 1: MAIN GUIDE

1. Identity

Full Name: Williams Percent Range

Abbreviation: Williams %R, %R

Category: Momentum / Oscillator

Creator: Larry Williams (1979)

One-Sentence Definition: Williams %R is a momentum oscillator that measures the current closing price relative to the highest high over a lookback period, oscillating between 0 and -100 to identify overbought and oversold conditions.

2. Calculation Logic

Formula

$$\%R = \frac{Highest\ High_N - Close_t}{Highest\ High_N - Lowest\ Low_N} \times -100$$

Where:

- $Highest\ High_N$ = Highest High over the last N periods

- $Lowest\ Low_N$ = Lowest Low over the last N periods

- $Close_t$ = Current closing price

StepLogicPurpose
1Find Highest High in N periodsUpper range boundary
2Find Lowest Low in N periodsLower range boundary
3Calculate range = HH - LLTotal price range
4Calculate position from topWhere close sits in range
5Multiply by -100Scale to 0 to -100

Real-World Analogy

Imagine climbing a ladder. Williams %R tells you how far you've dropped from the top rung. At 0, you're at the ceiling (overbought). At -100, you're at the floor (oversold).

7. Practical Cautions

Lag Characteristics

ComponentCharacteristic
TypeLeading indicator
First Valid ValuePeriod bars
Embedded Readings: In super-trends, %R can stay between 0 and -20 for weeks. Do NOT treat sustained overbought as automatic sell signal.
Division by Zero: If High == Low for entire lookback period, calculation fails.

Mitigation Strategies

- Trade the exit from zones, not the entry into zones

- Use failure swings for higher reliability

- Apply -50 midpoint as trend continuation filter

8. Pro Insights

Momentum Failure Detection

The most potent signal is NOT entering the overbought zone, but failure to reach it. If price makes a new high but %R stalls at -30 (failing to reach -20), a sharp sell-off is often imminent.

The -50 Midpoint Strategy

In confirmed downtrends, use -50 as resistance. When %R rallies but cannot cross above -50, bears remain in control—add to short positions.

9. Summary

"Williams %R measures how far you've dropped from the top—buy when you hit the floor, sell when you hit the ceiling."

PART 2: SUPPLEMENTARY GUIDE

1. Core Concept

Williams %R answers: "Where is the current close positioned within the recent trading range, measured from the top?"

At 0, the close is at the absolute high—maximum bullish extension. At -100, the close is at the absolute low—maximum bearish extension. The math is essentially identical to Fast Stochastic, just inverted.

2. Statistical Edge

Why It Works

- Prices tend to revert to mean after reaching extremes

- Readings beyond ±80 represent statistically significant events that tend to reverse

3. Actionable Strategy

Strategy Name: "Williams Pullback Entry"

Entry Rules:

1. Price is above 50-period SMA (Uptrend confirmed)

2. %R(14) drops into oversold zone (-80 to -100)

3. Enter when %R crosses back above -80

4. Confirm with volume spike

Exit Rules:

1. %R reaches overbought zone (-20 to 0)

2. OR trailing stop 2× ATR below entry

Position Sizing: 2% risk per trade

Signal Patterns

Oversold Bounce Signal

This signal triggers when %R crosses back above -80 after being in the oversold zone, indicating that selling pressure has exhausted and buyers are returning. The SMA(50) filter ensures trades are taken in the direction of the intermediate trend, dramatically improving win rates. Trading the exit from oversold (not the entry) avoids premature entries during trend continuations.


def williams_r_buy_signal(wr, price, sma50):
    return (
        wr[-1] > -80 and 
        wr[-2] <= -80 and  # Cross occurred
        price[-1] > sma50[-1]  # Uptrend filter
    )

Failure Swing Detection

The failure swing is one of the most potent %R signals—it occurs when the indicator enters oversold, rallies, pulls back but holds above -80, then rallies again. This higher-low pattern in momentum indicates buyers are absorbing selling pressure and building a base for reversal. Failure swings show significantly higher reliability than simple zone exits because they demonstrate genuine demand absorption.


def williams_r_failure_swing(wr):
    was_oversold = any(v < -80 for v in wr[-10:-5])
    higher_low = wr[-1] > -80 and wr[-3] < wr[-1]
    return was_oversold and higher_low
