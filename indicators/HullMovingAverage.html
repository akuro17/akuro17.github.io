Hull Moving Average

PART 1: MAIN GUIDE

1. Identity

Full Name: Hull Moving Average

Abbreviation: HMA

Category: Trend Following / Zero Lag

Creator: Alan Hull (2005)

One-Sentence Definition: The Hull Moving Average uses weighted moving averages and square-root smoothing to virtually eliminate lag while maintaining smoothness, creating a trend line that often appears to lead price action.

2. Calculation Logic

Formula

StepFormulaPurpose
Half WMA$WMA_{n/2}(Close)$Fast component
Full WMA$WMA_n(Close)$Slow component
Raw HMA$2 \times WMA_{n/2} - WMA_n$Lag compensation
Final HMA$WMA_{\sqrt{n}}(Raw)$Smooth overshoot

The Genius:

$$HMA_n = WMA\left(2 \times WMA(P, n/2) - WMA(P, n), \sqrt{n}\right)$$

Step-by-Step Logic:

1. Calculate WMA with half period ($n/2$)

2. Calculate WMA with full period ($n$)

3. Compute: $Raw = 2 \times WMA_{half} - WMA_{full}$

4. Apply final WMA smoothing with period $\sqrt{n}$

5. Result "hugs" price with minimal lag

Real-World Analogy

Imagine a race car's steering. Standard moving averages (SMA) are like driving a bus—slow to turn. The EMA is a sports car—faster but still some delay. The HMA is a Formula 1 car with zero-lag steering—the wheel turns precisely when you want it to, sometimes even anticipating the curve. This makes HMA appear to "lead" price at turning points.

7. Practical Cautions

Overshoot at Turns: HMA can swing BEYOND the high/low of candles at sharp reversals due to the lag-compensation math. This is mathematically correct but visually confusing.
Integer Square Root: The formula uses $\sqrt{n}$. This must be rounded to an integer. If $\sqrt{n} < 1$ (period = 1), code breaks. Minimum practical period ≈ 4.
Second Candle Confirmation: Wait for the SECOND bar of a new slope direction. Single-bar "hooks" often immediately reverse in choppy conditions.

8. Pro Insights

The "Hook" Entry: Because HMA overshoots slightly, the moment it flattens and hooks up (turns Green) is often the absolute bottom of a swing. This is the highest-probability entry point—but only in trending conditions. Never trade hooks in flat markets.

Slope as Signal, Not Line: Unlike traditional MAs where you watch for price crossovers, with HMA the slope change IS the signal. The Hook Up/Down is your entry, not "price crosses HMA."

Overshoot Advantage: HMA's mathematical overshoot at turns can actually work in your favor—it gives earlier signals than any other MA. The "lead" you perceive is the compensation term overshooting price momentarily.

MACD Confirmation Rule: HMA is fast but noisy. Adding MACD Histogram > 0 as a filter cuts false signals by 30%+ while maintaining most of the speed advantage.

9. Summary

"The Hull Moving Average is your zero-lag trend detector—when it turns, the market is turning, often before other indicators even notice."

PART 2: SUPPLEMENTARY GUIDE

1. Core Concept

The HMA answers: "Where is the trend RIGHT NOW, not where it was?"

Alan Hull solved the classic lag-vs-smoothness dilemma. By subtracting a slow WMA from twice a fast WMA, then smoothing the result, he created an average that eliminates nearly all lag while remaining smooth. It's the closest thing to a "future-looking" moving average that's mathematically possible.

2. Statistical Edge

The HMA provides:

- 2-3 bar earlier signals than equivalent-period EMA

- Slope change signals correct direction 65%+ in trending markets

- Can appear to "lead" price at turns (lag compensation overshoot)

- Tighter stop placement due to closer price following

3. Actionable Strategy: "HMA Hook Entry"


STRATEGY: Momentum Hook Trade
FILTER: ADX(14) > 20                   // Confirm trending
SETUP: HMA(16) was falling (Red)
TRIGGER: HMA(16) turns up (Green) for 2 consecutive bars
ENTRY: Close of second green bar
STOP: Low of the hook bar - 1× ATR
EXIT: HMA turns negative (Red) for 2 bars

4. Risks & Limitations

- Whipsaw in ranges: Too responsive in choppy markets

- Overshoot confusion: Can extend beyond candle extremes

- Period calculation edge cases: Very small periods cause issues

- Overconfidence in speed: Fast signals ≠ correct signals

Signal Patterns

HMA Hook Up Signal

This signal detects the precise moment when HMA transitions from falling to rising—the "hook" pattern. Due to HMA's zero-lag nature, this inflection point often coincides with (or slightly leads) the actual price bottom. In trending markets, hook signals correctly predict direction 65%+ of the time.


def hma_hook_up(hma, hma_prev, hma_prev2):
    was_falling = hma_prev < hma_prev2
    now_rising = hma > hma_prev
    return was_falling and now_rising

HMA Double Green Confirmation Signal

This pattern requires two consecutive "green" bars (rising HMA) to confirm the hook signal and filter out false single-bar reversals. In choppy markets, single-bar hooks frequently reverse immediately, but double confirmation reduces false signals by approximately 30% while maintaining most of the speed advantage that makes HMA valuable.


def hma_double_green(hma, hma_prev, hma_prev2):
    green_now = hma > hma_prev
    green_yesterday = hma_prev > hma_prev2
    return green_now and green_yesterday  # Confirmation
