Fisher Transform

PART 1: MAIN GUIDE

1. Identity

Full Name: Fisher Transform

Abbreviation: FISH

Category: Oscillator / Leading Indicator / DSP

Creator: John F. Ehlers (2002)

One-Sentence Definition: The Fisher Transform mathematically normalizes non-Gaussian price data into an approximate Gaussian distribution, creating sharp turning points that identify reversals with minimal lag.

2. Calculation Logic

Formula

StepFormulaPurpose
Normalize Price$X = 2 \times \frac{HL2 - L_n}{H_n - L_n} - 1$Map to -1 to +1 range
Smooth$X' = 0.5 \times X_{prev} + 0.5 \times X$Reduce noise
Transform$Fisher = 0.5 \times \ln\left(\frac{1 + X'}{1 - X'}\right)$Gaussian conversion
Signal Line$Trigger = Fisher_{t-1}$Crossover reference

Variables:

- $HL2$ = (High + Low) / 2 (Median Price)

- $H_n$, $L_n$ = Highest/Lowest $HL2$ over period $n$

- $X$ = Normalized value (-1 to +1)

Step-by-Step Logic:

1. Calculate HL2 (median price) for each bar

2. Find the highest and lowest HL2 over $n$ periods

3. Normalize current HL2 to range -1 to +1

4. Clamp to ±0.999 to prevent Log(0) errors

5. Apply smoothing: blend current with previous value

6. Apply Fisher formula: $\ln((1+X)/(1-X))$

7. Trigger line = previous Fisher value

Real-World Analogy

Imagine a metal detector at the beach. Normal oscillators (like RSI) give you a steady beeping that changes gradually. The Fisher Transform is like upgrading to a detector that screams loudly when you're directly over treasure and goes silent otherwise—no gradual buildup, just sharp "found it!" signals at turning points.

Why It Works: The Fisher Transform exploits the fact that market prices have "fat tails" (non-Gaussian). By mathematically forcing Gaussian distribution, extreme readings (outside ±2.0) become statistically rare events, making them highly predictive.

7. Practical Cautions

CRITICAL: Input Clamping: The formula uses $\ln((1+X)/(1-X))$. If $X$ reaches exactly 1.0 or -1.0, division by zero or undefined log occurs. Implementation MUST clamp X to ±0.999.
Neutral Zone Noise: Ignore crossovers occurring between -1.5 and +1.5. Only trade signals at extremes.
Pinned at Extremes: In parabolic moves, Fisher can stay above +2.0 for extended periods, generating multiple false "sell" signals. Use trend filters (ADX > 30 = avoid counter-trend Fisher signals).

8. Pro Insights

The Divergence Killer: Fisher Transform's sharp turning points make divergences exceptionally reliable. If price makes a "lazy" lower low but Fisher makes a sharp, distinct higher low (especially below -2.0), it's a "Spring" setup with high win rate.

Square Wave Visualization: Unlike sine-wave oscillators (RSI), Fisher looks more like a square wave with steep vertical walls. This makes state changes visually obvious—you're either "in" or "out" with little ambiguity.

Ehlers' Design Philosophy: John Ehlers is a DSP (Digital Signal Processing) engineer. The Fisher Transform applies mathematical signal processing to eliminate the noise that plagues traditional oscillators, creating "zero lag" at critical turning points.

PART 2: SUPPLEMENTARY GUIDE

1. Core Concept

The Fisher Transform answers: "How statistically extreme is the current price?"

Market data isn't normally distributed—it has fat tails and asymmetric skew. The Fisher Transform mathematically "normalizes" this into a Gaussian (bell curve) distribution. In this transformed space:

- Values outside ±2.0 represent ~2 standard deviations (rare events)

- Mean reversion becomes statistically predictable

- Turning points become mathematically sharp

2. Statistical Edge

The Fisher Transform provides:

- Earlier reversal detection than RSI/Stochastic (2-3 bars on average)

- Sharper signal clarity at extremes

- Divergences at extremes have 65%+ win rate in backtests

- The ±2.0 threshold crosses correctly identify reversals ~70% of the time in ranging markets

3. Actionable Strategy: "Extreme Reversal Entry"


STRATEGY: Fisher V-Bottom Catcher
SETUP: Fisher drops below -2.0 (extreme oversold)
TRIGGER: Fisher crosses ABOVE Trigger line
CONFIRMATION: Price forms bullish reversal candle (hammer/engulfing)
ENTRY: Close of confirmation candle
STOP: Low of the extreme bar minus 1× ATR
TARGET: Fisher reaches 0 (first target) or +1.5 (full target)

4. Risks & Limitations

- Parabolic blindness: Stays pinned at extremes during strong trends

- Log(0) crash risk: Must clamp input values

- Noisy at short periods: Period < 7 generates excessive signals

- Not for trend following: Counter-trend tool only

Signal Patterns

Extreme Buy Signal

This signal fires when the Fisher Transform reverses from extreme oversold territory (below -2.0). The combination of an extreme reading and a trigger line crossover indicates a high-probability V-bottom reversal. Statistical backtests show this pattern correctly identifies reversals approximately 70% of the time in ranging markets.


def fisher_extreme_buy(fisher, fisher_prev, trigger, trigger_prev):
    was_extreme = fisher_prev < -2.0
    crossed_up = fisher > trigger and fisher_prev <= trigger_prev
    return was_extreme and crossed_up

Divergence Buy Signal

This pattern detects bullish divergence where price makes a lower low but the Fisher Transform makes a higher low. When this divergence occurs in oversold territory (Fisher below -1.5), it indicates waning selling pressure and an imminent reversal. Fisher Transform divergences are particularly reliable due to the indicator's sharp turning points.


def fisher_divergence_buy(price_low, price_low_prev, fish, fish_prev):
    lower_low = price_low < price_low_prev
    higher_fish = fish > fish_prev
    oversold = fish_prev < -1.5
    return lower_low and higher_fish and oversold
