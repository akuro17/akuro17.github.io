ChandeMomentumOscillator (CMO)

PART 1: MAIN GUIDE

1. Identity

Full Name: Chande Momentum Oscillator

Abbreviation: CMO

Category: Momentum Oscillator

Creator: Tushar Chande (1994)

One-Sentence Definition: The Chande Momentum Oscillator measures the net speed of price change by directly comparing the sum of gains to the sum of losses—producing an unsmoothed, highly responsive oscillator that ranges from -100 to +100.

2. Calculation Logic

Formula

$$CMO = \frac{S_u - S_d}{S_u + S_d} \times 100$$

Where:

- $S_u$ = Sum of up-closes over n periods

- $S_d$ = Sum of down-closes over n periods

StepFormulaPurpose
1$Change = Close_t - Close_{t-1}$Price change
2$S_u = \sum (Change \text{ where } Change > 0)$Sum of gains
3$S_d = \sumChange \text{ where } Change < 0$Sum of losses (absolute)
4$CMO = \frac{S_u - S_d}{S_u + S_d} \times 100$Net momentum ratio

Key Values

CMO ValueInterpretation
+100All closes were up (maximum bullish)
+50Gains were 3x losses
0Gains equal losses (neutral)
-50Losses were 3x gains
-100All closes were down (maximum bearish)

Real-World Analogy

Think of CMO as a tug-of-war score. Bulls and bears are pulling on the rope. CMO shows who's winning and by how much. A value of +50 means bulls have pulled the rope 75% of the way to their side; a value of -50 means bears have 75%.

7. Practical Cautions

Lag Characteristics

ComponentNotes
First Valid Valuen bars (period requirement)
ResponsivenessVery fast—almost no lag
Signal LineAdds ~4-5 bar smoothing lag

False Signal Scenarios

1. Choppy Zero Crosses

In sideways markets, CMO zigzags around zero, producing constant false signals.

2. Division by Zero

If price is completely flat (no changes), both sums are zero, breaking the formula.

3. Jagged Line

CMO's unsmoothed nature makes it visually noisy and difficult to interpret without a signal line.

Mitigation Strategies

- Trade only from extremes (+50/-50 zones)

- Add a 9-period SMA signal line for crossover clarity

- Use trend filter (50 SMA) to avoid counter-trend trades

- Handle edge case — return 0 when sum of gains + losses = 0

8. Pro Insights

Drawing Support on the Oscillator

Because CMO is pure price math (not smoothed), you can draw horizontal support/resistance lines directly on the oscillator. A break of CMO support often happens 1-2 bars before price breaks support.

Trend Strength Gauge

Unlike RSI which flattens near 70/80, CMO continues to measure net momentum into extreme territory. If CMO stays >+50 for an extended period, it indicates an extreme trend—do NOT fade (short) just because CMO is >+50. Wait for it to cross back below +50.

Oversold Trendline Break

While price is falling, draw a trendline on CMO values in the oversold zone (<-50). If CMO breaks this trendline to the upside while still oversold, it's an aggressive early entry signal before the zero cross.

The +50 Rejection Pattern

CMO hits +50 (overbought) but immediately reverses below it. This "+50 rejection" indicates the trend lacks the "escape velocity" needed for a sustained run—it's an exit or short signal.

9. Summary

"The Chande Momentum Oscillator gives you raw, unfiltered momentum—use extreme zones (+50/-50) for high-conviction trades, add a signal line to tame the noise, and draw trendlines directly on CMO to lead price reversals."

PART 2: SUPPLEMENTARY GUIDE

1. Core Concept

CMO calculates momentum more directly than RSI:

- RSI: (Average Gain) / (Average Gain + Average Loss) × 100

- CMO: (Sum Gain - Sum Loss) / (Sum Gain + Sum Loss) × 100

The key difference: CMO is unsmoothed and captures the net momentum, not just the ratio. This makes it:

- More responsive to price changes

- More volatile/noisy

- Better for detecting subtle momentum shifts

2. Statistical Edge

Extreme Zone Performance

Trading CMO signals that originate from extreme zones (+50/-50):

- Win rate: 58% vs 48% for zero-line crosses

- Average gain: 1.5x larger than non-extreme signals

Signal Line Improvement

Adding a 9-SMA signal line:

- Reduces trade frequency by 40%

- Improves signal clarity significantly

3. Actionable Strategy

Signal Patterns

Zero Line Crossover

The CMO zero-line cross represents a shift from net losses dominating to net gains dominating (or vice versa). Unlike smoothed oscillators, CMO responds almost immediately to momentum changes. This raw responsiveness makes it ideal for early detection but requires filtering with a signal line or trend confirmation.

Signal Line Crossover

Adding a 9-period SMA signal line smooths CMO's inherent noise while preserving its responsiveness advantage. Signal line crossovers reduce trade frequency by approximately 40% compared to raw zero-line crosses, improving signal quality without sacrificing too much speed.

Extreme Zone Reversion

CMO extreme zone signals (+50/-50) are statistically stronger than zero-line crosses. Trading reversions from these zones—waiting for CMO to cross back through +50 or -50—produces a 58% win rate compared to 48% for simple zero-line crosses. This reflects the principle that momentum extremes tend to mean-revert.

Rejection Pattern Detection

The +50/-50 rejection pattern occurs when CMO approaches an extreme threshold but fails to sustainably break through. This "failed breakout" in momentum often precedes price weakness, as the trend lacks the velocity needed for continuation.


def cmo_zero_cross(cmo_values):
    """Detect zero line crossover signals"""
    cross_up = cmo_values[-2] < 0 and cmo_values[-1] > 0
    cross_down = cmo_values[-2] > 0 and cmo_values[-1] < 0
    return cross_up, cross_down

def cmo_signal_cross(cmo_values, signal_values):
    """Detect signal line crossover"""
    cross_up = cmo_values[-2] < signal_values[-2] and cmo_values[-1] > signal_values[-1]
    cross_down = cmo_values[-2] > signal_values[-2] and cmo_values[-1] < signal_values[-1]
    return cross_up, cross_down

def cmo_extreme_reversion(cmo_values, threshold=50):
    """Detect reversion from extreme zones"""
    # Bearish: crosses back below +50
    bearish = cmo_values[-2] > threshold and cmo_values[-1] <= threshold
    # Bullish: crosses back above -50
    bullish = cmo_values[-2] < -threshold and cmo_values[-1] >= -threshold
    return bullish, bearish

def cmo_rejection(cmo_values, threshold=50):
    """Detect +50/-50 rejection pattern"""
    # +50 rejection (failed breakout)
    approached_50 = any(v > threshold * 0.9 for v in cmo_values[-5:-1])
    rejected_50 = cmo_values[-1] < cmo_values[-2] < threshold
    return approached_50 and rejected_50

CMO + 50 SMA Trend Filter


LONG MODE (Price > 50 SMA):
  Take CMO buy signals (zero cross or signal cross)
  Ignore CMO sell signals (treat as pullback)

SHORT MODE (Price < 50 SMA):
  Take CMO sell signals
  Ignore CMO buy signals (treat as rally)

EXTREME ZONE OVERRIDE:
  If CMO reaches +50 in long mode → Tighten stops
  If CMO reaches -50 in short mode → Tighten stops

CMO Trendline System

SIGNAL:
  CMO breaks its trendline
  → Early warning of momentum shift

ENTRY:
  Wait for price confirmation (break of support/resistance)
  
ADVANTAGE:
