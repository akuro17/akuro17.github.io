ArnaudLegouxMovingAverage (ALMA)

PART 1: MAIN GUIDE

1. Identity

Full Name: Arnaud Legoux Moving Average

Abbreviation: ALMA

Category: Moving Average

Creators: Arnaud Legoux & Dimitris Kouzis-Loukas (2009)

One-Sentence Definition: ALMA is a Gaussian-weighted moving average that solves the classic smoothness vs. responsiveness dilemma—it's as smooth as an SMA but responds as quickly as an EMA.

2. Calculation Logic

Formula

$$w_i = \exp\left( - \frac{(i - \text{offset})^2}{2\sigma^2} \right)$$

$$ALMA = \frac{\sum_{i=1}^{N} (w_i \times P_{N-i})}{\sum_{i=1}^{N} w_i}$$

StepFormulaPurpose
1$m = offset \times (period - 1)$Calculate weight center position
2$s = period / \sigma$Calculate weight spread
3$w_i = e^{-((i-m)^2)/(2s^2)}$Gaussian weight for each bar
4$ALMA = \frac{\sum(w_i \times Price_i)}{\sum(w_i)}$Weighted average normalized

Parameters Explained

ParameterDefaultRangeEffect
Period95-100Window size (like SMA period)
Offset0.850.0-1.0Weight center (0=middle, 1=recent)
Sigma61-10Weight spread (lower=tighter)

Real-World Analogy

Imagine a spotlight on a stage. Offset determines where the spotlight is aimed (0.85 = aimed at the most recent action). Sigma determines how focused the beam is (low sigma = tight spotlight, high sigma = diffused light). ALMA shines its Gaussian "spotlight" mostly on recent prices while still considering the broader context.

7. Practical Cautions

Lag Characteristics

SettingApproximate Lag
Offset 0.852-3 bars less than equivalent EMA
Offset 0.99Near-zero lag (almost repainting)
Offset 0.50Similar lag to SMA

False Signal Scenarios

1. The Flat ALMA Trap

When price enters consolidation, ALMA flattens. Crossovers in this zone are noise—wait for ALMA to slope again.

2. Offset Too High (0.99)

At extreme offsets, ALMA hugs price so tightly it appears to repaint. Signals are premature and unreliable.

3. Wrong Timeframe Match

Fast ALMA (period 9) on daily charts is too sensitive. Match period to timeframe.

Mitigation Strategies

- Slope Confirmation: Never trade against the ALMA slope

- Close Requirement: Require full candle close beyond ALMA, not just wick touch

- Dual ALMA: Use two ALMAs (fast/slow) instead of price crossover

8. Pro Insights

The Offset Secret Weapon

The offset parameter is what makes ALMA special:

- 0.99: Near-repainting responsiveness (scalping bots)

- 0.85: Standard setting (most applications)

- 0.50: Center of Gravity effect (oscillator-like behavior)

Professional quants adjust offset based on volatility—higher offset in high-volatility environments, lower offset when markets are calm.

2-3 Bar Early Entry

With offset 0.90 and period 9, ALMA crossovers typically trigger 2-3 bars before an equivalent EMA crossover. This edge is significant for scalpers where entry timing determines profitability.

Touch-and-Bounce as Dynamic Support

In strong trends, price rarely touches the 50-SMA due to lag. But price frequently "kisses" ALMA. Professional traders use these touches as low-risk entry points: buy when price touches ALMA from above and forms a bullish candle.

Data Quality Requirement

ALMA applies Gaussian weights to *every* bar in the window. A single NaN or bad data point corrupts the entire calculation. Pre-process data to handle gaps before feeding to ALMA.

9. Summary

"ALMA is the Goldilocks moving average—not too laggy like SMA, not too noisy like EMA, but just right for capturing trends in volatile markets."

PART 2: SUPPLEMENTARY GUIDE

1. Core Concept

Traditional moving averages force a trade-off:

- SMA: Smooth but laggy

- EMA: Responsive but noisy (overshoots)

ALMA uses a Gaussian (bell curve) distribution to weight prices. Instead of equal weights (SMA) or exponential decay (EMA), ALMA creates a "hump" of weight that can be positioned anywhere in the lookback window.

The offset parameter shifts this hump. At 0.85, the peak weight sits at 85% of the way toward the most recent price—giving recent data much more influence while maintaining the smooth bell-curve shape that filters noise.

Think of it as a smart camera that follows a subject while applying smooth stabilization.

Why Gaussian Filtering Works

Gaussian filters are the mathematical optimum for removing white noise (random fluctuations) while preserving low-frequency signals (trends). This is proven signal processing theory applied to financial data.

Signal Patterns

Price Crossover Detection

When price crosses above ALMA, it indicates a shift from bearish to bullish bias—the Gaussian-filtered trend line has been breached. Conversely, price falling below ALMA signals bearish momentum. Due to ALMA's superior noise filtering, these crossovers are typically more reliable than traditional SMA or EMA crossovers, especially in volatile markets.

ALMA Bounce Detection

In established uptrends, price often "bounces" off ALMA as dynamic support. This pattern is identified when price touches ALMA (low within a small percentage of the ALMA value), then closes above it with a bullish candle. This provides low-risk entry points within the prevailing trend, leveraging ALMA's role as adaptive support/resistance.


def alma_crossover(price, alma_values):
    """Detect price crossing ALMA"""
    cross_up = price[-2] <= alma_values[-2] and price[-1] > alma_values[-1]
    cross_down = price[-2] >= alma_values[-2] and price[-1] < alma_values[-1]
    return cross_up, cross_down

def alma_bounce(price, alma_values, candles):
    """Detect bounce off ALMA as support"""
    touched = abs(candles[-1].low - alma_values[-1]) < (candles[-1].high - candles[-1].low) * 0.1
    bullish_candle = candles[-1].close > candles[-1].open
    above_alma = candles[-1].close > alma_values[-1]
    
    return touched and bullish_candle and above_alma
