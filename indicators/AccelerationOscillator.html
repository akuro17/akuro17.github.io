AccelerationOscillator (AC)

PART 1: MAIN GUIDE

1. Identity

Full Name: Acceleration Oscillator

Abbreviation: AC

Category: Momentum Oscillator

Creator: Bill Williams (1995)

One-Sentence Definition: The Acceleration Oscillator measures the acceleration (or deceleration) of market momentum—it's the second derivative of price, telling you when momentum itself is speeding up or slowing down.

2. Calculation Logic

Formula

$$\text{Median Price (MP)} = \frac{High + Low}{2}$$

$$\text{Awesome Oscillator (AO)} = SMA(MP, 5) - SMA(MP, 34)$$

$$\text{AC} = AO - SMA(AO, 5)$$

StepFormulaPurpose
1$MP = \frac{High + Low}{2}$Calculate median price (true center of bar)
2$ShortSMA = SMA(MP, 5)$Fast-moving average of median price
3$LongSMA = SMA(MP, 34)$Slow-moving average of median price
4$AO = ShortSMA - LongSMA$Awesome Oscillator (momentum)
5$AC = AO - SMA(AO, 5)$Acceleration of momentum

Real-World Analogy

If Price is distance, then AO (Awesome Oscillator) is velocity—how fast you're traveling. The AC is acceleration—whether you're pressing the gas pedal or the brake. Just as a car about to stop shows deceleration before velocity drops to zero, AC turns before momentum (AO) changes direction.

7. Practical Cautions

Lag Characteristics

ComponentMinimum Required Bars
First Valid AO34 bars
First Valid AC38 bars (~39th bar)
Signal Confirmation2-3 additional bars for color sequence

False Signal Scenarios

1. The Zero Line Trap

The most common mistake is treating zero line crosses as signals. They are NOT. A cross above zero only means momentum and acceleration are balanced—not that you should buy.

2. Single Color Bar

Entering on the first green bar instead of waiting for the sequence. You need 2 bars above zero or 3 bars below zero.

3. Alligator Sleeping

Taking AC signals when the Alligator lines are intertwined. The market is ranging, and all oscillator signals are noise.

Mitigation Strategies

- Always wait for the color sequence — No shortcuts

- Check Alligator status first — If sleeping, don't trade

- Combine with AO — Double confirmation reduces whipsaws

- Use on higher timeframes — Daily charts reduce noise significantly

8. Pro Insights

The Color Change Is Everything

Zero line crosses mean nothing. The true signal is the color change sequence. Bill Williams specifically designed this rule to prevent premature entries. If you're buying, you need consecutive green bars—not just AC being positive.

The "Tax" for Fighting Momentum

When AC is below zero (momentum is bearish), you need 3 green bars instead of 2 to buy. This extra bar is the "tax" you pay for trading against the prevailing momentum. It's Williams' way of ensuring the reversal has genuine follow-through.

The Early Warning System

AC is designed to be the earliest possible signal in Williams' system. The hierarchy is:

1. AC turns first — Acceleration changes

2. AO turns second — Momentum responds

3. Price turns last — The visible move begins

By the time most traders see a moving average cross, the AC signal happened 3-5 bars ago. This is how professional Williams traders capture the "meat" of moves.

Zone Trading Mastery

When both AC and AO are green and above zero, you're in the "Green Zone"—maximum bullish acceleration with bullish momentum. This is the highest-probability environment for aggressive buying. Conversely, both red and below zero is the "Red Zone" for shorting.

9. Summary

"AC is momentum's early warning system—it tells you the driver hit the brakes before the car starts slowing down."

PART 2: SUPPLEMENTARY GUIDE

1. Core Concept

Think of price movement in three layers:

1. Position (Price itself) — Where is the car?

2. Velocity (AO - Awesome Oscillator) — How fast is the car going?

3. Acceleration (AC) — Is the driver pressing gas or brake?

AC measures the third layer. Before a car stops, it must decelerate first. Before momentum (AO) changes direction, it must slow down first. AC captures this slowing-down phase, giving you advance notice of momentum shifts.

The Math: AC is simply the difference between the current AO and its 5-period average. If AO is rising faster than its recent average, AC is positive (accelerating up). If AO is falling faster than its recent average, AC is positive down (accelerating down).

2. Statistical Edge

Why It Works

AC provides an edge because it identifies momentum shifts before they become visible in price. Studies show:

MetricAO Signals OnlyAC + AO Signals
Early Entry (bars)0 (baseline)2-3 bars earlier
False SignalsHigherReduced 20-30%
Risk/Reward1:1.51:2.0

The Physics Argument

Markets, like physical systems, cannot reverse instantly. There's always a deceleration phase. By tracking this deceleration (AC turning before AO), traders gain a mathematical edge in timing entries.

Academic Context

Bill Williams' work draws from chaos theory and nonlinear dynamics. While not academically peer-reviewed in the traditional sense, his 1995 book *Trading Chaos* has influenced a generation of momentum traders.

3. Actionable Strategy

Strategy Name: "AC-AO Zone Entry"

Concept: Enter when both AC and AO confirm the same direction, creating a "Zone" of maximum probability.

Setup Requirements:

1. Alligator is spreading (not sleeping)

2. Price is above (for longs) or below (for shorts) the Alligator

Entry Trigger (Long):

- AO bar is green (AO > previous AO)

- AC bar is green (AC > previous AC)

- Both AO and AC are above zero

Position Sizing:

- Full size in Green Zone (both green, both above zero)

- Half size if AC is green but AO is mixed

Exit Rules:

1. Primary Exit: AC prints a red bar (acceleration reverses)

2. Stop Loss: Below the most recent down Fractal

3. Trailing: Move stop to each new Fractal low

Example:


Day 1: AO = +0.52 (green), AC = +0.08 (green), both > 0 → GREEN ZONE
Day 2: Confirm zone still holds
Day 3: ENTRY at market open

Trade runs until AC prints first red bar (even if still > 0)

Psychological Traps

1. FOMO on Zero Cross

Seeing AC cross above zero feels urgent—but it's not a signal! Wait for the color bars.

2. Impatience with 3-Bar Rule

When AC is below zero, waiting for 3 green bars feels agonizing. Trust the system.

3. Overtrading the Colors

Every color change is not a trade. You're looking for *sequences*, not single bars.

Common Misinterpretations

- "AC > 0 means buy" → Wrong; you need the color sequence

- "Red bar means sell immediately" → Context matters; where is AC relative to zero?

- "AC works alone" → It's designed for the Williams system with Alligator

Signal Patterns

Buy Signal (Above Zero)

When the AC is positive (above zero) and displays two consecutive rising bars (today higher than yesterday, yesterday higher than the day before), this indicates that bullish momentum is accelerating. This is Bill Williams' aggressive buy signal, best used when the Alligator is already in "feeding" mode confirming an uptrend.


def ac_buy_above_zero(ac_values):
    """Aggressive buy: 2 consecutive green bars above zero"""
    ac_today = ac_values[-1]
    ac_yesterday = ac_values[-2]
    ac_2_days = ac_values[-3]
    
    above_zero = ac_today > 0
    green_bar_1 = ac_today > ac_yesterday
    green_bar_2 = ac_yesterday > ac_2_days
    
    return above_zero and green_bar_1 and green_bar_2

Buy Signal (Below Zero)

When the AC is negative (below zero) but shows three consecutive rising bars, this signals that bearish momentum is decelerating and a potential reversal is building. The extra bar requirement (3 instead of 2) acts as a "tax" for trading against the prevailing momentum, ensuring the reversal has genuine follow-through before entry.


def ac_buy_below_zero(ac_values):
    """Conservative buy: 3 consecutive green bars below zero"""
    ac_today = ac_values[-1]
    ac_yesterday = ac_values[-2]
    ac_2_days = ac_values[-3]
    ac_3_days = ac_values[-4]
    
    below_zero = ac_today < 0
    green_bar_1 = ac_today > ac_yesterday
    green_bar_2 = ac_yesterday > ac_2_days
    green_bar_3 = ac_2_days > ac_3_days
    
    return below_zero and green_bar_1 and green_bar_2 and green_bar_3

Divergence Signal

When price makes a lower low but the AC makes a higher low, this bullish divergence indicates that selling pressure is exhausting despite new price lows. This pattern often precedes significant reversals as it shows acceleration turning positive before momentum (AO) and price follow.


def ac_bullish_divergence(price, ac_values, lookback=10):
    """Price Lower Low + AC Higher Low = Bullish Divergence"""
    price_low_1 = min(price[-lookback:-lookback//2])
    price_low_2 = min(price[-lookback//2:])
    
    ac_low_1 = min(ac_values[-lookback:-lookback//2])
    ac_low_2 = min(ac_values[-lookback//2:])
    
    price_lower_low = price_low_2 < price_low_1
    ac_higher_low = ac_low_2 > ac_low_1
    
    return price_lower_low and ac_higher_low
