AwesomeOscillator (AO)

PART 1: MAIN GUIDE

1. Identity

Full Name: Awesome Oscillator

Abbreviation: AO

Category: Momentum

Creator: Bill Williams (1995)

One-Sentence Definition: The Awesome Oscillator measures market momentum by comparing recent price action (5-bar) to longer-term price action (34-bar), displayed as a histogram that shows whether buyers or sellers are in control.

2. Calculation Logic

Formula

$$\text{Median Price} = \frac{High + Low}{2}$$

$$AO = SMA(\text{Median Price}, 5) - SMA(\text{Median Price}, 34)$$

StepFormulaPurpose
1$MP_t = \frac{H_t + L_t}{2}$Calculate median price (bar midpoint)
2$FastMA = SMA(MP, 5)$5-period moving average
3$SlowMA = SMA(MP, 34)$34-period moving average
4$AO = FastMA - SlowMA$Momentum (fast minus slow)

Why Median Price?

Williams used median price instead of close because it represents the "true center" of trading activity—where price balanced between buyers and sellers during the bar.

Real-World Analogy

AO is like a momentum gauge on a speedboat. Positive values mean you're accelerating forward (buyers winning). Negative values mean you're going backward (sellers winning). The color of each bar shows if momentum is increasing (green) or decreasing (red).

Zero Line Cross


IF AO crosses from negative to positive
THEN → BUY signal (Fast MA crossed above Slow MA)

The Saucer Signal


IF AO > 0
AND AO[1] < AO[2]    // 1st red bar
AND AO[0] > AO[1]    // green bar (trigger)
THEN → BUY (Momentum dip recovered)

Twin Peaks Signal


IF AO < 0
AND has two troughs (peaks)
AND 2nd trough > 1st trough
AND current bar is green
AND no zero cross between peaks
THEN → BUY (Momentum building for reversal)

7. Practical Cautions

Lag Characteristics

ComponentNotes
First Valid AO34 bars (slow period)
Zero Line CrossLags price turns by 2-5 bars

False Signal Scenarios

1. Choppy Zero Crosses

In ranges, AO oscillates around zero repeatedly—each cross is noise.

2. Incomplete Twin Peaks

Entering before the green trigger bar confirms the pattern.

3. Crossing Zero Between Peaks

Twin Peaks is invalid if AO touches zero between the two troughs/peaks.

Mitigation Strategies

- Wait for pattern completion — Never anticipate

- Use with Alligator — If Alligator is sleeping, ignore AO

- Higher timeframes — Daily charts produce cleaner signals

8. Pro Insights

The Saucer Is NOT a Cup

The "Saucer" pattern only requires 3 bars:

1. Bar 1: Red bar (lower than previous)

2. Bar 2: Red bar (lower than Bar 1)

3. Bar 3: Green bar (higher than Bar 2) → ENTRY

This is a mini-pullback within a trend, not a major reversal pattern.

Twin Peaks Clean Rule

The histogram must not touch zero between the two peaks. If it does, the pattern is invalid. This ensures both peaks occurred within the same momentum phase.

Bill Williams' Advice

"When AO crosses zero on a daily chart, it means that the short-term momentum (this week's sentiment) has overtaken the long-term momentum (this month's sentiment). That's not a minor event."

Early Exit Signal

Once in a trade, exit when AO changes color against your position:

- Long position → Exit on first red bar

- Short position → Exit on first green bar

This locks in profits before momentum fully reverses.

9. Summary

"The Awesome Oscillator is Bill Williams' market momentum meter—green above zero means bulls are winning the momentum battle, red below zero means bears have the upper hand. Watch for Saucers and Twin Peaks for high-probability entries."

PART 2: SUPPLEMENTARY GUIDE

1. Core Concept

AO is essentially a moving average oscillator (like MACD) but uses:

1. Median price instead of close

2. Simple moving averages instead of exponential

3. 5/34 periods (Fibonacci numbers)

Positive AO: Short-term traders are more bullish than long-term traders

Negative AO: Short-term traders are more bearish than long-term traders

2. Statistical Edge

Pattern Performance

PatternWin RateAvg Gain

Saucer58%1.8%
Twin Peaks62%2.4%

Twin Peaks has highest reliability because it represents a divergence pattern occurring in extreme territory.

3. Actionable Strategy

Strategy Name: "AO Twin Peaks Reversal"

Setup (Bullish):

1. AO below zero

2. AO makes a low, recovers, makes a higher low

3. No zero cross between lows

4. Green bar forms after second low

Entry: Buy at close of green bar after 2nd low

Stop: Below the second trough's corresponding price low

Exit: When AO crosses below zero OR first red bar after profit target

Signal Patterns

Zero Line Cross Detection

When AO crosses from negative to positive, it signals that short-term momentum (5-period) has overtaken long-term momentum (34-period)—a shift in the underlying market sentiment. Bill Williams considered this a significant event indicating that "this week's sentiment has overtaken this month's sentiment," though it should be confirmed with a green bar.


def ao_zero_cross(ao_values):
    """Detect zero line crossover"""
    cross_up = ao_values[-2] < 0 and ao_values[-1] > 0
    cross_down = ao_values[-2] > 0 and ao_values[-1] < 0
    return cross_up, cross_down

Saucer Buy Pattern

The Saucer occurs when AO is above zero and shows a specific 3-bar sequence: two consecutive red bars (declining) followed by a green bar (rising). This mini-pullback within the bullish zone represents a brief momentum dip that has recovered, providing a high-probability re-entry point in the direction of the prevailing trend.


def ao_saucer_buy(ao_values):
    """Saucer buy signal above zero"""
    above_zero = ao_values[-1] > 0
    bar3_green = ao_values[-1] > ao_values[-2]
    bar2_red = ao_values[-2] < ao_values[-3]
    
    return above_zero and bar3_green and bar2_red

Twin Peaks Buy Pattern

The Twin Peaks formation is the most reliable AO signal. It occurs when AO is below zero and forms two troughs where the second trough is higher than the first (momentum building), followed by a green bar confirmation. The key rule: AO must not cross zero between the two peaks, ensuring both formed within the same bearish momentum phase.


def ao_twin_peaks_buy(ao_values, lookback=20):
    """Twin peaks bullish signal below zero"""
    below_zero = all(x < 0 for x in ao_values[-lookback:])
    
    # Find two troughs
    lows = []
    for i in range(1, len(ao_values)-1):
        if ao_values[i] < ao_values[i-1] and ao_values[i] < ao_values[i+1]:
            lows.append((i, ao_values[i]))
    
    if len(lows) >= 2:
        second_higher = lows[-1][1] > lows[-2][1]
        green_bar = ao_values[-1] > ao_values[-2]
        return below_zero and second_higher and green_bar
    return False
