Zero Lag Exponential Moving Average (ZLEMA)

PART 1: MAIN GUIDE

1. Identity

Full Name: Zero Lag Exponential Moving Average

Abbreviation: ZLEMA

Category: Trend / Moving Average

Creator: John Ehlers and Ric Way (circa 2010)

One-Sentence Definition: The Zero Lag Exponential Moving Average is a modified EMA designed to eliminate lag by tracking "de-lagged" data, calculated as Price + (Price - Price[Lag]), rather than raw price.

2. Calculation Logic

Formula

Step 1: Calculate Lag Component

$$Lag = \frac{N - 1}{2}$$

Step 2: Create De-Lagged Data

$$Data_{Adjusted} = 2 \times Price_t - Price_{t-Lag}$$

Step 3: Apply EMA to Adjusted Data

$$ZLEMA_t = \alpha \times Data_{Adjusted} + (1 - \alpha) \times ZLEMA_{t-1}$$

Where:

$$\alpha = \frac{2}{N + 1}$$

StepLogicPurpose
1Calculate lag offsetHalf the period minus 1
2De-lag the priceAdd momentum projection
3Apply EMA smoothingFinal ZLEMA value

Real-World Analogy

Think of ZLEMA like a GPS navigator that predicts where you'll be based on your current speed and direction. If you're driving north at 60mph, it doesn't just show where you are—it anticipates where you're heading. This "projection" eliminates the delay of waiting for your actual position to update.

7. Practical Cautions

Lag Characteristics

ComponentCharacteristic
LagNear-zero in linear trends
OvershootHigh in sharp reversals
Overshoot Problem: ZLEMA projects the current trend forward. In sharp V-reversals, it continues pointing the old direction for several bars before snapping back.

Mitigation Strategies

- Always use a higher-timeframe trend filter (e.g., 200 EMA)

- Wait for price to cross *back* over ZLEMA, don't trade the tip

- Use dual ZLEMA crossover for confirmation

8. Pro Insights

The Overshoot Warning

Because ZLEMA extrapolates data, it will often "overshoot" on sharp reversals. If price spikes up and stops, ZLEMA might continue rising for one more bar. Professional traders wait for price to cross *back* through ZLEMA rather than acting on the first touch.

Premier Trailing Stop

ZLEMA's tight tracking makes it an excellent trailing stop in parabolic markets. In crypto or high-momentum tech stocks, using ZLEMA(14) as a trailing stop prevents giving back too much profit.

Slope Color Strategy

Many platforms color ZLEMA based on slope (green = rising, red = falling). Simple strategy: Only hold longs when ZLEMA is green, only hold shorts when ZLEMA is red.

9. Summary

"ZLEMA trades speed for stability—it gets you in first, but watch out for the overshoot."

PART 2: SUPPLEMENTARY GUIDE

1. Core Concept

The term "Zero Lag" is technically a simplification. What ZLEMA achieves is near-zero lag during sustained linear trends by adding momentum to the price before applying the EMA calculation.

The formula 2×Price - Price[Lag] essentially says: "If the current trend continues at the same rate, where will price be?" This projected value is then smoothed by the EMA. During straight trends, this projection is accurate and lag vanishes. During reversals, the projection is wrong and causes overshoot.

2. Statistical Edge

Why It Works

- In trending markets, momentum persists (autocorrelation)

- The de-lagging term (Price - Price[Lag]) captures this momentum

- Early entries in trends compound returns significantly

Trade-off

Edge in trending markets offset by more false signals in choppy markets.

Signal Patterns

ZLEMA Slope Turn Buy Signal

This signal fires when ZLEMA's slope transitions from negative to positive, indicating that the momentum projection has shifted from bearish to bullish. Due to ZLEMA's de-lagging calculation, slope turns occur near the actual price inflection point rather than bars later as with standard EMAs. However, traders should be aware of the overshoot problem—in V-reversals, ZLEMA may continue its old direction briefly before snapping back.


def zlema_slope_turn(zlema):
    current_slope = zlema[-1] - zlema[-2]
    previous_slope = zlema[-2] - zlema[-3]
    return previous_slope < 0 and current_slope > 0

Trend Recapture Pattern

This pattern identifies high-probability entries when price pulls back below ZLEMA and then recaptures above it within an established uptrend (price above 200 EMA). The pullback-and-recapture sequence confirms that the dip was a temporary correction rather than a trend reversal. This "trend recapture" strategy is particularly effective in momentum-driven markets like crypto and high-growth tech stocks.


def zlema_recapture(price, zlema, ema200):
    pullback_occurred = price[-2] < zlema[-2]
    recaptured = price[-1] > zlema[-1]
    in_uptrend = price[-1] > ema200[-1]
    return pullback_occurred and recaptured and in_uptrend
