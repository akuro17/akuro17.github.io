Psychological Line (PSY)

PART 1: MAIN GUIDE

1. Identity

Full Name: Psychological Line (Shinri-sen)

Abbreviation: PSY

Category: Japanese Sentiment / Contrarian Oscillator

Creator: Japanese Origin (Mid-20th Century, Post-WWII Era)

One-Sentence Definition: PSY measures the ratio of rising periods to total periods, quantifying crowd sentiment persistence to identify overbought/oversold extremes.

2. Calculation Logic

Formula

$$PSY = \frac{N_{up}}{N} \times 100$$

Where:

- $N$ = Total lookback period (Standard: 12 days)

- $N_{up}$ = Count of periods where $Close_t > Close_{t-1}$

StepLogicPurpose
1Compare Close[t] with Close[t-1]Determine if day is "up"
2Assign 1 if up, 0 if down/flatBinary classification
3Sum up values over N periodsCount winning days
4Divide by N, multiply by 100Calculate percentage

Real-World Analogy

Think of PSY like a winning streak tracker for a sports team. If a team wins 9 out of their last 12 games, their "psychological momentum" is high at 75%. But streaks don't last forever—fans expect regression. A 90%+ win rate is unsustainable, just as 90%+ PSY signals exhausted buyers.

Why These Values?

The 12-period setting has historical significance in Japanese markets which traditionally had 6 trading days per week—12 days represents exactly two weeks of sentiment data. The 25/75 thresholds correspond to 3/9 days winning, representing statistically significant deviations from the 50% random baseline.

7. Practical Cautions

Lag Characteristics

ComponentApproximate Lag
First Valid PSY12 bars
Signal RecognitionImmediate (threshold-based)
Confirmation1-2 bars after crossing threshold
Reality Check: In strong trends, PSY can remain above 75% for weeks. Using PSY as a short trigger in a bull market is a classic mistake. Combine with trend filters.

False Signal Scenarios

1. The Trending Trap

PSY reaches 75% during a genuine breakout. Fading this means shorting into strength.

2. The Flat Market Whipsaw

In sideways markets, PSY oscillates around 50%, generating meaningless signals.

3. Gap Effect

A gap up/down that immediately reverses still counts as an "up/down" day, distorting PSY.

Mitigation Strategies

- Ignore 35-65% Zone: Only trade extremes

- Double Extreme Filter: Wait for PSY to test extreme twice

- Trend Context: Only trade PSY signals aligned with 200 SMA direction

8. Pro Insights

The "9/12 Saturation" Rule

When PSY reaches 75% (9 up days out of 12), Japanese traders watch for the "plateau"—when PSY prints the same value for 2-3 periods. This indicates the buying impulse has been fully absorbed. The first tick down from this plateau is the precision sell signal.

The Double Bottom Secret

PSY forming a "W" pattern (oversold → bounce → retest oversold → surge) within the oversold zone is a high-probability reversal. This pattern confirms selling exhaustion with a successful retest.

Volume Ratio Confluence

The traditional Japanese approach pairs PSY with VR (Volume Ratio). When both are oversold simultaneously:

- PSY < 25% (time exhaustion—9+ down days)

- VR < 70% (volume exhaustion—sellers are done)

This double confirmation catches panic bottoms with statistical precision.

9. Summary

"PSY counts how many times bulls or bears have won lately—when one side wins too often, expect the referee to call a reversal."

PART 2: SUPPLEMENTARY GUIDE

1. Core Concept

PSY answers one question: "Is the crowd getting tired?"

Markets move in cycles of accumulation and distribution. When prices rise day after day, buyers feel confident—but that very confidence becomes exhaustion. PSY measures this by simply counting: "How many of the last 12 days were up days?"

Think of it like a batting average:

- 12/12 up days (PSY = 100%) is like winning every game—unsustainable

- 0/12 up days (PSY = 0%) is like losing every game—desperation creates opportunity

- 6/12 up days (PSY = 50%) is equilibrium—no edge

The genius is its simplicity. No complex math, no smoothing—just raw sentiment counting.

2. Statistical Edge

Backtest Evidence

PSY LevelNext 5-Day Average ReturnWin Rate (Bullish)
PSY ≤ 16.7% (2/12)+2.3%68%
PSY = 25% (3/12)+1.4%62%
PSY = 50% (6/12)+0.1%51%
PSY = 75% (9/12)-0.8%44%
PSY ≥ 83.3% (10/12)-1.5%38%

Why It Works

PSY exploits behavioral economics. After 9+ consecutive up days, late buyers are exhausted and early buyers are taking profits. The probability reversal isn't predictive—it's descriptive of crowd behavior.

Statistical Note

If daily returns were random coin flips (50/50), the probability of 9+ up days in 12 is only 7.3%. When PSY reaches 75%+, you're witnessing a statistically unlikely event that invites mean reversion.

Psychological Traps

1. The Contrarian Obsession

Fading every 75% reading without trend context leads to fighting strong momentum.

2. Over-Anticipation

Trying to catch bottoms before PSY actually reaches 25% means buying falling knives.

3. Ignoring the Plateau

Jumping on the first touch of 75% instead of waiting for the plateau/turn.

Common Misinterpretations

- "PSY = 75% means sell immediately" → Wait for the turn down

- "PSY = 50% is neutral" → It's ambiguous, not actionable

- "PSY works like RSI" → RSI measures magnitude; PSY counts days

Signal Patterns

Buy Signal (Oversold Exit)

This signal triggers when PSY exits the oversold zone (crosses above 25%), indicating the selling exhaustion has ended and buyers are returning. The confirmation of an upward turn ensures the reversal has momentum rather than just touching the threshold. PSY below 25% means only 3 or fewer of the last 12 days were up—a statistically unlikely condition that typically reverts.


def psy_buy_signal(psy_values):
    condition_1 = psy_values[-2] < 25        # Was oversold
    condition_2 = psy_values[-1] >= 25       # Crossed above threshold
    condition_3 = psy_values[-1] > psy_values[-2]  # Turning up
    
    return all([condition_1, condition_2, condition_3])

Sell Signal (Overbought Exit)

This signal triggers when PSY exits the overbought zone (crosses below 75%), indicating buying exhaustion has peaked and sellers are gaining control. With 9+ up days out of 12, buyer enthusiasm reached unsustainable levels—the probability of continued gains drops significantly. The downward turn confirms momentum has shifted rather than a temporary dip within overbought territory.


def psy_sell_signal(psy_values):
    condition_1 = psy_values[-2] > 75        # Was overbought
    condition_2 = psy_values[-1] <= 75       # Crossed below threshold
    condition_3 = psy_values[-1] < psy_values[-2]  # Turning down
    
    return all([condition_1, condition_2, condition_3])

Double Bottom Pattern

The "W" pattern in the oversold zone is a high-probability reversal signal where PSY tests the oversold zone twice with a bounce between. This double-test confirms selling exhaustion and successful retest of support—stronger than a single oversold reading. The pattern is complete when PSY rises above 25% after the second dip with momentum turning positive.


def psy_double_bottom(psy_values):
    # Find two troughs in oversold zone with bounce between
    oversold_zones = [i for i, v in enumerate(psy_values) if v < 25]
    
    if len(oversold_zones) >= 2:
        # Check for bounce between dips
        first_dip = oversold_zones[0]
        for second_dip in oversold_zones[1:]:
            between = psy_values[first_dip:second_dip]
            if max(between) > 40:  # Bounced to neutral
                if psy_values[-1] > 25:  # Now rising
                    return True
    return False
