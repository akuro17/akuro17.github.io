Yang-Zhang Volatility Estimator

PART 1: MAIN GUIDE

1. Identity

Full Name: Yang-Zhang Volatility Estimator

Abbreviation: YZ Volatility, YZ

Category: Volatility / Statistical

Creator: Dennis Yang and Qiang Zhang (2000)

One-Sentence Definition: The Yang-Zhang Volatility Estimator is a sophisticated statistical measure of historical volatility that incorporates overnight gaps and intraday ranges, providing drift-independent accuracy even in strongly trending markets.

2. Calculation Logic

Formula

The Yang-Zhang estimator combines three variance components:

Component 1: Overnight Variance (σ²_open)

$$\sigma^2_{open} = \frac{1}{N-1} \sum_{i=1}^{N} \left( \ln \frac{O_i}{C_{i-1}} \right)^2$$

Component 2: Open-to-Close Variance (σ²_close)

$$\sigma^2_{close} = \frac{1}{N-1} \sum_{i=1}^{N} \left( \ln \frac{C_i}{O_i} \right)^2$$

Component 3: Rogers-Satchell Variance (σ²_RS)

$$\sigma^2_{RS} = \frac{1}{N} \sum_{i=1}^{N} \left[ \ln \frac{H_i}{O_i} \cdot \ln \frac{H_i}{C_i} + \ln \frac{L_i}{O_i} \cdot \ln \frac{L_i}{C_i} \right]$$

Final Yang-Zhang Variance:

$$\sigma^2_{YZ} = \sigma^2_{open} + k \cdot \sigma^2_{close} + (1-k) \cdot \sigma^2_{RS}$$

Weighting Constant k:

$$k = \frac{0.34}{1.34 + \frac{N+1}{N-1}}$$

Real-World Analogy

Think of Yang-Zhang as a complete health check versus a simple pulse reading. Close-to-close volatility only checks your pulse. Yang-Zhang checks overnight sleep (gaps), workout intensity (intraday range), and resting heart rate (drift-adjusted movement).

7. Practical Cautions

Lag Characteristics

ComponentCharacteristic
TypeStatistical measure (non-directional)
First Valid ValuePeriod + 1 bars
Logarithm Failures: The formula requires ln(O/C_{prev}). If any price is 0 or negative, calculation returns NaN.
Non-Directional: High volatility doesn't tell you which way—only that a big move is happening.

Mitigation Strategies

- Validate all prices are positive before calculation

- Use gap-adjustment for missing bars

- Apply rolling window with overlap to smooth edge effects

8. Pro Insights

The Volatility Cycle

Markets "breathe"—they inhale (low volatility, consolidation) and exhale (high volatility, trending). Yang-Zhang is the best tool to identify the "holding breath" moment (squeeze) that precedes explosive moves.

Bracket Order Strategy

When YZ hits multi-year lows, place both a buy-stop above resistance and a sell-stop below support. The volatility expansion will trigger one direction.

Position Sizing Integration

Use YZ directly for position sizing: Size = Fixed $ Risk / (YZ% × Price). This ensures consistent dollar risk across different volatility regimes.

PART 2: SUPPLEMENTARY GUIDE

1. Core Concept

Traditional volatility measures (Close-to-Close standard deviation) ignore intraday information and fail when markets gap overnight. Yang and Zhang's innovation (2000) combined three volatility components:

1. Overnight variance — capturing gap risk

2. Open-to-Close variance — measuring session movement

3. Rogers-Satchell variance — using High/Low for efficiency

The weighting constant k is mathematically optimized to minimize the variance of the estimator itself.

2. Statistical Edge

Why It Works

- Captures overnight gap risk that simpler estimators miss

- Drift-independent: Accurate in trending and ranging markets

- Higher efficiency: Converges with fewer data points

Academic Foundation

Dennis Yang and Qiang Zhang, *"Drift Independent Volatility Estimation Based on High, Low, Open, and Close Prices"* (Journal of Business, 2000).

Signal Patterns

Volatility Squeeze Detection

This signal triggers when YZ volatility drops to the 5th percentile or below over a 100-day lookback, indicating an extreme compression in market movement—the "inhale" before a major trend "exhale." Markets cycle between low and high volatility regimes, and extreme squeezes historically precede explosive directional moves. The direction of the breakout is unknown, but the probability of a significant move is extremely high.


def yz_squeeze(yz_vol, lookback=100):
    percentile = stats.percentileofscore(yz_vol[-lookback:], yz_vol[-1])
    return percentile < 5

YZ Band Breakout

This breakout signal fires when price exceeds a volatility-based band constructed as MA plus 2× YZ volatility (percentage), indicating a statistically significant move beyond normal price distribution. Unlike fixed bands, YZ-based bands adapt to true market volatility including overnight gaps and intraday ranges. Breakouts above the upper band suggest the start of an uptrend, particularly when volatility is expanding from a squeeze condition.


def yz_band_breakout(price, ma, yz_vol, multiplier=2):
    upper_band = ma[-1] + (yz_vol[-1] / 100) * price[-1] * multiplier
    return price[-1] > upper_band
