Directional Movement Index

PART 1: MAIN GUIDE

1. Identity

Full Name: Directional Movement Index

Abbreviation: DMI

Category: Trend / Momentum System

Creator: J. Welles Wilder Jr. (1978)

One-Sentence Definition: The Directional Movement Index is a comprehensive trend analysis system that decomposes price movement into positive and negative directional components, answering both "Is there a trend?" and "Which direction is it going?"

2. Calculation Logic

Formula

StepFormulaPurpose
1$\Delta H = High_t - High_{t-1}$Upward expansion
2$\Delta L = Low_{t-1} - Low_t$Downward expansion
3$+DM = \Delta H$ if $\Delta H > \Delta L$ and $\Delta H > 0$, else $0$Positive directional movement
4$-DM = \Delta L$ if $\Delta L > \Delta H$ and $\Delta L > 0$, else $0$Negative directional movement
5$TR = \max(H-L,H-C_{prev},L-C_{prev})$True Range
6$+DI = \frac{Smoothed(+DM)}{Smoothed(TR)} \times 100$Positive Directional Indicator
7$-DI = \frac{Smoothed(-DM)}{Smoothed(TR)} \times 100$Negative Directional Indicator

Wilder's Smoothing:

$$Smoothed_t = Smoothed_{t-1} - \frac{Smoothed_{t-1}}{N} + Value_t$$

Real-World Analogy

Think of DMI like a tug-of-war referee. The bulls and bears are constantly pulling on opposite ends of the rope. +DM measures how much rope the bulls gained today (new high territory), while -DM measures the bears' gains (new low territory). The +DI and -DI lines show who's winning the overall match, and the ADX (derived from DMI) tells you how intense the competition is.

Why These Values?

Wilder designed the 14-period setting to represent approximately two trading weeks, providing enough smoothing to filter noise while remaining responsive to genuine trend changes. The 25 threshold represents the statistical boundary where directional movement becomes significant.

7. Practical Cautions

Lag Characteristics

ComponentApproximate Lag
First Valid +DI/-DI~14 bars (Period)
Stable Wilder Smoothing~28 bars (2×Period)
DI Crossover Signal3-5 bars after trend begins
Reality Check: DI crossovers are lagging signals. By the time +DI crosses above -DI, the initial breakout move is often complete. Use DMI for trend *confirmation* rather than early entry.

False Signal Scenarios

1. Low ADX Whipsaws

When ADX < 20, DI lines cross frequently without meaningful trends. These signals are noise.

2. Single-Bar Spikes

A single high-volume bar can cause temporary DI spike without sustainable trend.

3. ADX Trend Chasing

ADX already above 50 when you enter = late to the party; exhaustion near.

Mitigation Strategies

- Mandatory ADX Filter: Never trade DI crossovers when ADX < 25

- ADX Slope Rule: ADX should be rising at crossover, not falling

- Confirmation Bar: Wait one bar after crossover to confirm

8. Pro Insights

The ADX Hook Pattern

When ADX spikes above 50 (extreme trend strength) and then "hooks" downward, the trend is exhausting. This is NOT a reversal signal, but a warning to:

- Stop adding to positions

- Tighten trailing stops

- Prepare for consolidation or reversal

The Failure Swing

Wilder's favorite advanced signal: If ADX is rising and +DI makes a high, drops, then makes a *lower high* while price makes a *higher high*, the trend is internally weak despite the price appearance. This divergence often precedes significant reversals.

DI Spread for Trend Scoring

Calculate DI_Spread = abs(+DI - (-DI)):

- High spread (>20): Strong directional dominance

- Low spread (<10): Neutral, no edge for trend strategies

Use DI_Spread to rank stocks for momentum portfolios.

The Perfect Setup

The ideal DMI setup combines:

1. ADX rising from below 20 to above 25 (trend birth)

2. +DI crossing above -DI at the same time

3. Price breaking above recent resistance

4. Volume confirmation

9. Summary

"DMI is your trend's tug-of-war scoreboard—+DI shows the bulls' gains, -DI shows the bears', and ADX tells you if the match is worth watching."

PART 2: SUPPLEMENTARY GUIDE

1. Core Concept

DMI answers two fundamental questions:

1. "Is there a trend?" — Answered by ADX level

2. "Which direction?" — Answered by +DI vs -DI comparison

The genius of Wilder's system is separating direction from strength. Most indicators confuse these concepts. DMI cleanly partitions daily price ranges into "bullish expansion" (+DM) and "bearish expansion" (-DM), then normalizes by volatility (True Range) to create comparable readings across different assets.

The True Range normalization is crucial: without it, high-volatility stocks would show inflated directional readings compared to low-volatility stocks. By dividing by TR, both a $10 stock and a $500 stock produce similar DI ranges.

Psychological Traps

1. Ignoring ADX Levels

The most common mistake is trading DI crossovers without checking ADX first.

2. ADX > 50 Euphoria

High ADX feels bullish, but often signals exhaustion, not continuation.

3. Waiting for "Perfect" Setup

By the time ADX > 25 + DI cross + price confirmation align, significant move is done.

Common Misinterpretations

- "High ADX = Strong Buy" → ADX measures strength, not direction

- "+DI > -DI always means buy" → Only valid when ADX > 25

- "ADX predicts direction" → ADX is non-directional

Signal Patterns

Trend Entry (DI Crossover with ADX Filter)

This signal detects when the +DI line crosses above the -DI line, indicating bulls are gaining directional control. The ADX > 25 filter ensures the market is actually trending, avoiding whipsaw signals during range-bound conditions. Combining directional crossover with trend strength confirmation significantly improves signal reliability.


def dmi_buy_signal(plus_di, minus_di, adx, plus_di_prev, minus_di_prev):
    di_cross = plus_di > minus_di and plus_di_prev <= minus_di_prev
    adx_confirms = adx > 25
    
    return di_cross and adx_confirms

Trend Exhaustion

This pattern identifies when an extreme trend (ADX > 50) begins losing momentum, signaling potential consolidation or reversal ahead. When ADX "hooks" downward from extreme levels, it warns traders to stop adding positions and tighten stops. This is not a reversal signal but an exhaustion alert indicating the trend's strongest phase has passed.


def dmi_exhaustion(adx, adx_prev, adx_threshold=50):
    extreme_reached = adx_prev > adx_threshold
    turning_down = adx < adx_prev
    
    return extreme_reached and turning_down

Trend Birth

This signal captures the powerful moment when a dormant market transitions into an active trend. It detects ADX crossing above 25 (from below) while the DI spread shows clear directional dominance. Trend births often precede significant price moves as the market shifts from consolidation to directional momentum.


def dmi_trend_birth(adx, adx_prev, plus_di, minus_di):
    adx_rising = adx > adx_prev
    crossing_threshold = adx_prev < 25 and adx >= 25
    direction_clear = abs(plus_di - minus_di) > 10
    
    return adx_rising and crossing_threshold and direction_clear
