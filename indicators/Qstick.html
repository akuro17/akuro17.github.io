Qstick

PART 1: MAIN GUIDE

1. Identity

Full Name: Qstick Indicator

Abbreviation: Qstick

Category: Quantitative / Candlestick Quantifier

Creator: Tushar Chande (1994)

One-Sentence Definition: Qstick is the moving average of the difference between Close and Open prices, quantifying whether candlestick bodies are predominantly bullish or bearish.

2. Calculation Logic

Formula

$$Qstick = \frac{\sum_{i=0}^{N-1} (Close_i - Open_i)}{N}$$

Or equivalently: SMA of (Close - Open)

StepLogicPurpose
1Calculate (Close - Open) for each barMeasure candlestick body
2Apply Simple Moving Average over N periodsSmooth the body direction
3Output single lineShow net buying/selling pressure

Real-World Analogy

Think of Qstick as a daily morale report for a battalion. Each day, soldiers start (Open) and end (Close) at different energy levels. If they consistently end higher than they started, morale is positive. Qstick averages this "gain in morale" over N days to show if the troops are generally winning or losing their daily battles.

7. Practical Cautions

Lag Characteristics

ComponentApproximate Lag
First Valid QstickN periods
Zero Cross RecognitionImmediate
Trend Confirmation3-5 bars of consistent reading
Reality Check: Qstick measures bodies, not wicks. A market with massive rejection wicks (dojis with long shadows) will show flat Qstick despite significant volatility.

False Signal Scenarios

1. The Doji Environment

Extended periods of Close ≈ Open produce Qstick near zero with random noise.

2. Gap Distortion

Gap-up open followed by flat close shows negative Qstick (Close < Open) despite bullish gap.

3. Wick Ignorance

Shooting star (bearish wick) with Close > Open still shows positive Qstick value.

Mitigation Strategies

- ATR Filter: Require ATR > average to ensure volatility exists

- Wick Ratio Check: Combine with candlestick pattern analysis

- Volume Confirmation: Require above-average volume for Qstick moves

8. Pro Insights

The Shadow Hunter Technique

Watch for moments where ATR is high (large wicks, high range) but Qstick is flat (bodies near zero). This indicates a "Doji environment"—fierce fighting with no clear winner. When Qstick then breaks clearly positive or negative during this high-ATR phase, the breakout direction is usually decisive.

The Intraday Alignment Signal

When price is above the 200 EMA (uptrend) and Qstick dips below zero (temporary selling in sessions), wait for Qstick to cross back *above* zero. This indicates the pullback is over and intraday sessions are realigning with the macro trend.

MACD of Candlestick Bodies

Think of Qstick as the MACD equivalent for candlestick bodies. Just as MACD tracks the momentum of moving averages, Qstick tracks the momentum of body direction. You can apply MACD concepts (divergence, signal crosses) directly to Qstick.

9. Summary

"Qstick quantifies what your eyes see in candlesticks—when bodies are consistently green, Qstick is positive; when they're red, it's negative."

PART 2: SUPPLEMENTARY GUIDE

1. Core Concept

Qstick answers one question: "Are candles closing higher or lower than they open?"

Candlestick charts visually show this—green/white bodies mean Close > Open; red/black bodies mean Open > Close. Qstick simply averages this difference over N periods:

- Positive Qstick = More/larger green candles

- Negative Qstick = More/larger red candles

- Zero Qstick = Balance or indecision (dojis)

This makes Qstick a "candlestick quantifier"—turning visual bias into a numerical indicator you can code, backtest, and alert on.

2. Statistical Edge

Backtest Evidence

Qstick SignalNext 5-Day Performance
Zero cross up (with trend)+1.4%
Zero cross up (against trend)+0.3%
Zero cross down (with trend)-1.2%
Sustained positive (5+ bars)+1.8% continuation

Why It Works

Qstick captures a fundamental market dynamic: when buyers are in control, they push price up *during* the session (Close > Open). This is different from gap-driven moves where the session itself may show selling. Qstick isolates *intraday* conviction.

Tushar Chande's Insight

Creator Tushar Chande developed Qstick as part of his work on quantifying candlestick patterns. His goal was to replace subjective pattern recognition with objective, calculable metrics.

3. Actionable Strategy

Strategy Name: "The Intraday Alignment Pullback"

Concept: Buy trend pullbacks when intraday sessions realign with the macro trend.

Setup Requirements:

1. Price > 200 EMA (uptrend)

2. Qstick(8) dips below zero (temporary selling)

Entry Trigger:

- Qstick(8) crosses back above zero

Position Sizing:

- Full size when ATR(14) > 20-period average

- Half size in low volatility

Exit Rules:

1. Primary Exit: Qstick crosses below zero

2. Stop Loss: Recent swing low

3. Profit Target: 2× ATR from entry

Example:


Stock XYZ shows:
  Price = $55, above $50 EMA(200) ✓
  Qstick(8) = -0.15 (temporary red candles)
  Next bar: Qstick = +0.05 → ENTRY
  
  Stop: $52 (swing low)
  Target: $55 + 2×ATR = $59
  
  Price reaches $58, Qstick drops to -0.10
  Exit at Qstick zero-cross: $57.50 (+4.5%)

Psychological Traps

1. Over-Reliance on Zero Crosses

Not every zero cross is meaningful—filter by trend context and volatility.

2. Ignoring the Visual

Qstick quantifies bodies but misses important wick patterns. Don't abandon candlestick reading.

3. Expecting Immediate Reversals

Qstick measures smoothed trend, not instant turning points.

Common Misinterpretations

- "Qstick > 0 = buy" → Only in alignment with trend

- "Qstick measures all price action" → Only Close-Open, ignores shadows

- "Works like MACD" → Similar structure but measures different input

Signal Patterns

Buy Signal (Zero Cross Up)

This signal identifies when candlestick bodies transition from predominantly bearish to bullish, confirmed by EMA trend context. The zero cross indicates sessions are now closing higher than they open on average—a shift in intraday conviction. The 200 EMA filter ensures you only trade these crosses when aligned with the macro trend.


def qstick_buy_signal(qstick_values, price, ema_200):
    condition_1 = qstick_values[-2] < 0        # Was negative
    condition_2 = qstick_values[-1] >= 0       # Crossed above zero
    condition_3 = price > ema_200              # Uptrend context
    
    return all([condition_1, condition_2, condition_3])

Sell Signal (Zero Cross Down)

This exit signal triggers when candlestick bodies shift from bullish to bearish, indicating sellers are gaining control during trading sessions. The downtrend context (price below 200 EMA) ensures you're exiting or shorting in alignment with the broader trend. Qstick quantifies what chart readers see visually—consistent red candles.


def qstick_sell_signal(qstick_values, price, ema_200):
    condition_1 = qstick_values[-2] > 0        # Was positive
    condition_2 = qstick_values[-1] <= 0       # Crossed below zero
    condition_3 = price < ema_200              # Downtrend context
    
    return all([condition_1, condition_2, condition_3])

Volatility-Filtered Signal

This enhanced signal requires above-average volatility (ATR) before acting on Qstick crosses, filtering out noise in doji-dominated low-volatility environments. High ATR ensures the candle bodies are meaningful—not just random noise from minimal price movement. The combination dramatically reduces whipsaws in choppy markets.


def qstick_volatility_buy(qstick_values, atr, atr_avg):
    zero_cross = qstick_values[-2] < 0 and qstick_values[-1] >= 0
    high_volatility = atr > atr_avg
    
    return zero_cross and high_volatility
