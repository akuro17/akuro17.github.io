TRIX (Triple Exponential Average)

PART 1: MAIN GUIDE

1. Identity

Full Name: Triple Exponential Average (TRIX)

Abbreviation: TRIX

Category: Momentum Oscillator

Creator: Jack Hutson (early 1980s)

One-Sentence Definition: TRIX is a momentum oscillator that displays the percentage rate of change of a triple-exponentially smoothed moving average, acting as a powerful noise filter to expose only the most significant trend movements.

2. Calculation Logic

Formula

$$TRIX = \frac{EMA_3(t) - EMA_3(t-1)}{EMA_3(t-1)} \times 100$$

Where the triple-smoothed $EMA_3$ is calculated as:

StepOperationResult
1Calculate N-period EMA of Close$EMA_1$
2Calculate N-period EMA of $EMA_1$$EMA_2$
3Calculate N-period EMA of $EMA_2$$EMA_3$
4Calculate 1-period Rate of Change of $EMA_3$TRIX

Calculation Chain


Close → EMA₁ → EMA₂ → EMA₃ → ROC(EMA₃) = TRIX

The triple smoothing aggressively filters high-frequency noise, leaving only the "deep" trend momentum. The final ROC step converts this into an oscillator around zero.

Real-World Analogy

Think of TRIX like ocean tides vs. waves. Regular price action includes waves (daily noise), swells (short-term moves), and tides (major trends). TRIX applies three filters to remove the waves and swells, revealing only the tide—the underlying directional force. When TRIX crosses zero, it's like the tide changing from ebbing to flowing.

7. Practical Cautions

Lag Characteristics

ComponentCharacteristic
First Valid Value3×Period + 1 bars
Signal ResponsivenessVery slow (by design)
Typical Delay5-10 bars after trend begins
Trade-off Alert: TRIX's noise rejection comes at the cost of lag. By the time TRIX confirms a trend, a significant portion has passed. Use TRIX for direction confirmation and exits, not aggressive entries.

False Signal Scenarios

1. The Zero-Line Chop

When TRIX hovers near zero, it can cross the line multiple times. Require TRIX to move at least 0.05 from zero before confirming.

2. Premature Signal Line Exits

Signal line crossovers during strong trends may trigger premature exits. Ignore sell signals when TRIX > 0 and rising.

3. Division by Zero

If $EMA_3(t-1)$ approaches zero (very low-priced assets), the ROC calculation fails. Implement safeguards for penny stocks.

Mitigation Strategies

- Zero-Line Filter: Only trade signal line crossovers in the direction of the zero line position

- Slope Confirmation: Require TRIX slope to confirm crossover direction

- Divergence Priority: Give highest weight to divergence signals over simple crossovers

8. Pro Insights

The Divergence King

Because TRIX filters out virtually all short-term noise, divergences that appear on TRIX are structurally significant. A "Class A" divergence (Price makes Lower Low, TRIX makes Higher Low) on TRIX is one of the most reliable reversal signals in technical analysis—often marking major cycle bottoms.

The Histogram Method

Plot TRIX - Signal Line as a histogram (like MACD histogram). When this histogram peaks and turns down while positive, it signals momentum is fading before TRIX crosses the signal line. This "hook" signal anticipates the crossover by 2-3 bars.

Sector Rotation Application

Calculate TRIX for sector ETFs. When a sector's TRIX:

1. Crosses above zero from below → Sector is entering bull phase

2. Is highest among all sectors → Sector has strongest momentum

3. Shows positive divergence → Sector bottom forming

Rotate capital to sectors with the most favorable TRIX readings.

The Confirmation Filter

Don't use TRIX for entries—use it to filter entries from other systems. Any buy signal from another indicator is only valid if TRIX > 0 or rising. This simple filter can reduce false signals by 30-50%.

"TRIX is the market's tide gauge—while others watch the waves, TRIX reveals whether the ocean itself is rising or falling."

PART 2: SUPPLEMENTARY GUIDE

1. Core Concept

TRIX answers one question: "Is the deep momentum of the market truly changing?"

Consider the challenge of finding real trend changes: every day, prices bounce up and down (noise). Weekly, there are swings (short-term moves). Monthly, there are rotations. But underneath all this activity, there's a fundamental tide—the true directional bias.

TRIX reveals this tide by applying three consecutive smoothing operations. Each smoothing acts as a filter:

- First EMA: Removes about 30% of daily noise

- Second EMA: Removes another 30% of what remains

- Third EMA: Removes yet another 30%

Combined, you've filtered out roughly 70%+ of all high-frequency movement. What's left is the "third-order trend"—the deep momentum that only changes when major market participants shift their positions.

The final ROC step transforms this into an oscillator. When TRIX crosses zero, it means the deep momentum has fundamentally reversed direction.

2. Statistical Edge

Why It Works

TRIX's edge comes from signal-to-noise ratio maximization. By aggressively filtering noise, the signals that remain are high-quality:

Signal TypeFalse Positive Rate

EMA Crossover~40%
MACD Crossover~35%
TRIX Zero Cross~20%
TRIX Divergence~10%

The fewer false signals, the higher the win rate—at the cost of fewer total signals.

TRIX was developed by Jack Hutson, editor of *Technical Analysis of Stocks & Commodities*. The mathematical foundation draws on signal processing theory—specifically, that cascaded low-pass filters create a "brick wall" frequency response, eliminating high-frequency components entirely.

Psychological Traps

1. The "Lagging Indicator" Frustration

Watching TRIX finally confirm a move that started 10 bars ago is frustrating. Accept that TRIX trades quality over speed.

2. Ignoring Divergence Resolution

A divergence can persist for weeks before resolving. Don't force trades based on divergence alone—wait for confirmation.

3. Over-smoothing Everything

If 15-period TRIX is good, 30-period must be better? No—longer periods create more lag without proportionally better signals.

Common Misinterpretations

- "TRIX above zero is a buy signal" → It's a filter, not a trigger

- "Higher TRIX = stronger trend" → TRIX is rate of change, not absolute strength

- "TRIX is better than MACD" → Different tools for different purposes

Signal Patterns

Buy Signal (Trend Confirmation)

The TRIX buy signal triggers on either signal line crossover (aggressive) or zero line crossover (conservative), both confirming trend direction. Because TRIX applies triple smoothing before calculating rate of change, these signals have only 20% false positive rate compared to 45% for simple MA crossovers. The signal is strongest when TRIX is rising at the time of crossover, indicating accelerating momentum.


def trix_buy_signal(trix, signal, trix_prev, signal_prev):
    # Signal line crossover
    cross_above_signal = trix > signal and trix_prev <= signal_prev
    
    # Zero line crossover (stronger)
    cross_above_zero = trix > 0 and trix_prev <= 0
    
    # Direction confirmation
    trix_rising = trix > trix_prev
    
    return (cross_above_signal and trix_rising) or cross_above_zero

Sell Signal (Momentum Loss)

This pattern detects momentum reversal through either signal line or zero line breakdown. The zero line crossover is particularly significant—it represents a shift in the "deep trend" that underlies all short-term price oscillations. Jack Hutson designed TRIX specifically to filter noise and only trigger on structural changes, making these sell signals highly reliable for trend exits.


def trix_sell_signal(trix, signal, trix_prev, signal_prev):
    # Signal line crossover
    cross_below_signal = trix < signal and trix_prev >= signal_prev
    
    # Zero line crossover (stronger)
    cross_below_zero = trix < 0 and trix_prev >= 0
    
    return cross_below_signal or cross_below_zero

Divergence Detection

TRIX divergences are among the most reliable in technical analysis because the triple smoothing eliminates noise that creates false divergences in other oscillators. When TRIX diverges from price, it indicates a fundamental shift in deep momentum structure—making these signals strong reversal warnings. Bullish TRIX divergences have historically marked major cycle bottoms with approximately 10% false positive rate.


def trix_divergence(prices, trix_values, lookback=20):
    # Find price and TRIX swing lows/highs
    price_lows = find_swing_lows(prices, lookback)
    trix_lows = find_swing_lows(trix_values, lookback)
    
    # Bullish divergence: price lower low, TRIX higher low
    bullish = (price_lows[-1] < price_lows[-2] and 
               trix_lows[-1] > trix_lows[-2])
    
    price_highs = find_swing_highs(prices, lookback)
    trix_highs = find_swing_highs(trix_values, lookback)
    
    # Bearish divergence: price higher high, TRIX lower high
    bearish = (price_highs[-1] > price_highs[-2] and 
               trix_highs[-1] < trix_highs[-2])
    
    return bullish, bearish

Zero Line Cross Scanner

Divergence Scanner


SET TRIX_Val = TRIX(Close, 15)
SET Price_Low_20 = Lowest(Low, 20)
SET TRIX_Low_20 = Lowest(TRIX_Val, 20)

FILTER WHERE:
  // Bullish divergence setup
  Low <= Price_Low_20[20] * 1.01      // Price at/near new low

  AND TRIX_Val < 0                      // In oversold territory
