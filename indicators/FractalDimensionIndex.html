Fractal Dimension Index

PART 1: MAIN GUIDE

1. Identity

Full Name: Fractal Dimension Index

Abbreviation: FDI

Category: Market State / Volatility / Chaos Theory

Creator: Benoit Mandelbrot (Theory) / Carlos Sevcik & Alex Matulich (Algorithm)

One-Sentence Definition: The Fractal Dimension Index measures the geometric complexity of price movement to distinguish between trending markets (linear) and ranging markets (chaotic), acting as a regime filter rather than a directional signal.

2. Calculation Logic

Formula

StepFormulaPurpose
NormalizeMap prices to unit square (0-1 range)Standardization
Path Length$L = \sum \sqrt{(\Delta Time)^2 + (\Delta Price)^2}$Total price "distance"
FDI$FDI = 1 + \frac{\ln(L) + \ln(2)}{\ln(2N)}$Dimension estimate

Interpretation:

- $FDI = 1.0$ → Straight line (perfect trend)

- $FDI = 1.5$ → Critical threshold (trend/range boundary)

- $FDI = 2.0$ → Plane-filling noise (pure chaos)

Step-by-Step Logic:

1. Calculate sum of absolute price differences over $N$ periods

2. Find the highest high and lowest low in the window

3. Compute price range (highest - lowest)

4. Apply dimension formula with logarithms

5. Clamp result between 1.0 and 2.0

Real-World Analogy

Imagine looking at a coastline from space. A smooth beach is linear (FDI ≈ 1.0)—you could walk it in a straight line. A jagged fjord coastline is complex (FDI ≈ 2.0)—every zoom reveals more zigzags. The FDI tells you whether the market's "coastline" is smooth (trending) or jagged (choppy).

3. Market Standards

ParameterValuePurpose

Trend Zone1.0 - 1.5Use trend-following tools

Why 1.5? This is the mathematical midpoint between 1D (line) and 2D (plane). Below 1.5, prices move efficiently in one direction. Above 1.5, prices fill space randomly.

4. Market Context

Best: Regime filter to select appropriate indicators

Good: Identifying breakout potential (high FDI → potential breakout)

Worst: Using as standalone directional signal (FDI has NO direction)

Critical Understanding:

FDI tells you "Trending or Ranging?" but NOT "Up or Down?"

It must be paired with directional indicators to trade.

6. Filter Usage

This is FDI's primary purpose—as a filter, not a signal generator.

FDI StateIndicator Selection
FDI < 1.5Enable: Moving Averages, MACD, Trend lines
FDI < 1.5Disable: RSI/Stochastic overbought/oversold
FDI > 1.5Enable: RSI, Stochastic, Mean reversion
FDI > 1.5Disable: MA crossovers (will whipsaw)

The "Indicator Switch" Rule


IF FDI > 1.5:
    → Ignore all Moving Average signals
    → Trade Support/Resistance bounces instead
    
IF FDI < 1.5:
    → Ignore RSI overbought/oversold signals
    → Trade breakouts and trend continuations

7. Practical Cautions

Not a Directional Indicator: FDI ONLY tells "Trend" or "Consolidation." It has NO information about direction. Always pair with price action or trend indicators.
Zero Range Division: If High == Low over period (market is completely flat), the calculation fails (division by zero). Code must return neutral value (1.5).
Computational Cost: FDI calculation is O(N) per bar—more expensive than simple indicators. On large datasets, may slow backtesting.

8. Pro Insights

The "Indicator Switch": Don't use FDI to generate buy/sell signals. Use it to turn other indicators ON/OFF:

- FDI > 1.5 → Disable trend indicators, enable oscillators

- FDI < 1.5 → Disable oscillators, enable trend indicators

Breakout Anticipation: When FDI reaches extreme highs (>1.7), the market is "storing energy" in a tight range. A downturn from these highs often precedes explosive breakouts. Combine with Bollinger Band squeeze for high-probability setups.

Trend Fragility Warning: If FDI drops to 1.1 or 1.2 (near straight line), the trend is "too perfect"—parabolic. Markets rarely move in straight lines for long. This extreme signals imminent reversal.

9. Summary

"The FDI is your market state thermometer—it doesn't tell you hot or cold (direction), but whether you're in a solid (trend) or liquid (range) state, so you know which tools to use."

PART 2: SUPPLEMENTARY GUIDE

1. Core Concept

The FDI answers: "Is the market moving efficiently (trending) or randomly (ranging)?"

Based on Mandelbrot's fractal geometry, FDI measures how "complex" the price path is. A straight move from A to B has dimension 1.0. Random wandering that fills the chart has dimension 2.0. The market oscillates between these states.

2. Statistical Edge

The FDI provides:

- Objective regime classification (no subjective interpretation)

- Eliminates 30-40% of losing MA crossover trades by filtering ranges

- Breakouts from FDI > 1.7 have 60%+ follow-through rate

- More reliable than ADX for distinguishing trend/range

3. Actionable Strategy: "Regime-Based Trading"


STRATEGY: Fractal Trend Confirmation
FILTER: FDI(30) drops below 1.5        // Market entering trend mode
DIRECTION: SuperTrend indicator        // Determines long/short

IF FDI < 1.5 AND SuperTrend == Bullish:
    BUY with SuperTrend stop
    
IF FDI rises above 1.5:
    EXIT immediately (regime changed)

4. Risks & Limitations

- No direction: Must combine with directional indicators

- Lag at transitions: Takes several bars to confirm regime change

- Computational intensity: Slower than simple indicators

- Period sensitivity: Too short = noise; too long = late signals

Signal Patterns

FDI Trend Start Signal

This signal fires when FDI crosses below the critical 1.5 threshold, indicating a transition from chaotic/ranging market to trending market. By confirming direction with price above SMA, traders can identify the formation of tradeable trends. This regime shift eliminates 30-40% of losing MA crossover trades by filtering out range-bound conditions.


def fdi_trend_start(fdi, fdi_prev, price, sma):
    crossed_below = fdi < 1.5 and fdi_prev >= 1.5
    direction_up = price > sma
    return crossed_below and direction_up

FDI Breakout Setup Signal

This pattern detects when the market transitions from extreme randomness (FDI > 1.7) to more organized movement. High FDI values indicate the market is "storing energy" in consolidation, and a downturn from these extremes often precedes explosive breakouts. Breakouts from this setup have a 60%+ follow-through rate.


def fdi_breakout_setup(fdi, fdi_prev):
    was_extreme = fdi_prev > 1.7
    falling = fdi < fdi_prev
    return was_extreme and falling  # Energy release imminent
