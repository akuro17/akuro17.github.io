AverageTrueRange (ATR)

PART 1: MAIN GUIDE

1. Identity

Full Name: Average True Range

Abbreviation: ATR

Category: Volatility

Creator: J. Welles Wilder Jr. (1978)

One-Sentence Definition: ATR measures pure market volatility—the average "breathing room" price needs each period—making it essential for position sizing, stop-loss placement, and volatility-based strategies.

2. Calculation Logic

Formula

$$TR = \max(High - Low, |High - Close_{prev}|, |Low - Close_{prev}|)$$

$$ATR_t = \frac{ATR_{t-1} \times (n-1) + TR_t}{n}$$

StepFormulaPurpose
1$TR_1 = High - Low$First bar True Range
2$TR_t = \max(H-L,H-C_{prev},L-C_{prev})$Account for gaps
3$ATR_1 = SMA(TR, n)$First ATR is simple average
4$ATR_t = \frac{ATR_{t-1} \times (n-1) + TR_t}{n}$Wilder smoothing

Why True Range?

ScenarioSimple RangeTrue Range
Normal day$H - L$$H - L$
Gap up$H - L$ (misses gap)$H - C_{prev}$ (captures gap)
Gap down$H - L$ (misses gap)$L - C_{prev}$ (captures gap)

Real-World Analogy

ATR is like a weather forecast for market turbulence. An ATR of $2.50 on a $100 stock means "expect daily swings of about 2.5%." Just as you'd prepare differently for a calm day vs. a storm, traders adjust stops and position sizes based on ATR.

Position Sizing Formula


Risk Amount = Account × Risk%
Position Size = Risk Amount / (Entry - Stop)
              = Risk Amount / (N × ATR)

Example:
  Account = $100,000
  Risk = 1% = $1,000
  ATR = $2.00
  Stop = 2× ATR = $4.00
  Position Size = $1,000 / $4.00 = 250 shares

Common Mistakes

1. Using ATR for Signals

ATR measures volatility, not direction. Don't buy because "ATR is high."

2. Ignoring Price Normalization

ATR of $5 means nothing without context. Use ATR/Close for comparison.

3. Fixed ATR Multiples

1.5× ATR stop might be too tight in volatile markets, too wide in quiet ones.

8. Pro Insights

Volatility Normalization

Smart traders don't look for "high ATR"—they normalize:

$$Normalized\ ATR = \frac{ATR}{Close} \times 100$$

Normalized ATRClassificationUse Case
> 5%High volatility "Mover"Day trading
2-5%Medium volatilitySwing trading
< 2%Low volatility "Grinder"Position trading

ATR for Position Sizing Across Assets

To risk the same dollar amount on Apple ($150) and Amazon ($3000):

- Apple ATR = $3 → Stop = $6 → Position = $1000/$6 = 166 shares

- Amazon ATR = $60 → Stop = $120 → Position = $1000/$120 = 8 shares

This "volatility-adjusted" sizing prevents the high-priced stock from dominating portfolio swings.

Extreme ATR = Reversal Warning

When ATR spikes to 3-4× its average, it often marks exhaustion:

- Crash spikes frequently mark bottoms (panic selling exhausted)

- Rally spikes often mark tops (euphoric buying exhausted)

Look for price reversals following extreme ATR readings.

9. Summary

"ATR is not a signal—it's your market weather report. It doesn't tell you where to go, but how much turbulence to expect on the journey. Use it to size your positions and place your stops."

PART 2: SUPPLEMENTARY GUIDE

1. Core Concept

Before ATR, traders used High-Low range for volatility. But this ignored gaps. Wilder's innovation: True Range captures the full daily movement including overnight gaps.

If a stock closes at $100, gaps up to $105, then trades $105-$108:

- Simple Range = $108 - $105 = $3

- True Range = $108 - $100 = $8 (captures the gap)

ATR smooths True Range over N periods for a stable volatility reading.

Signal Patterns

ATR Breakout Filter

When ATR exceeds its simple moving average, volatility is expanding—confirming that a price breakout has genuine momentum behind it. Breakouts occurring during rising ATR are statistically more likely to continue than those during falling ATR, as expanding volatility indicates increasing market participation.


def atr_breakout_filter(atr, atr_sma):
    """Validate breakout with ATR expansion"""
    return atr[-1] > atr_sma[-1]

ATR Squeeze Detection

When ATR reaches its lowest point over the lookback period, the market is in a volatility compression phase—a "squeeze" that typically precedes explosive moves. This low-volatility environment stores potential energy that will eventually release, though ATR alone cannot predict the direction of the breakout.


def atr_squeeze(atr, period=20):
    """Detect volatility squeeze"""
    return atr[-1] == min(atr[-period:])

ATR Stop-Loss Calculation

Using ATR to calculate stop-losses ensures that stops are calibrated to actual market volatility. A 2× ATR stop provides statistically significant breathing room while limiting risk. This method automatically widens stops in volatile markets and tightens them in quiet markets, adapting to changing conditions.


def atr_stop_loss(entry, atr, multiplier=2.0, direction='long'):
    """Calculate ATR-based stop"""
    if direction == 'long':
        return entry - (multiplier * atr)
    else:
        return entry + (multiplier * atr)
