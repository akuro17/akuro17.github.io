Coppock Curve

PART 1: MAIN GUIDE

1. Identity

Full Name: Coppock Curve

Abbreviation: CC

Category: Momentum Oscillator (Long-Term)

Creator: Edwin Sedgwick Coppock (1962)

One-Sentence Definition: The Coppock Curve is a weighted moving average of combined rates of change, designed to identify major buying opportunities following significant market corrections by measuring the "end of mourning" in investor psychology.

2. Calculation Logic

Formula

$$\text{Coppock Curve} = \text{WMA}_{10}(\text{ROC}_{14} + \text{ROC}_{11})$$

StepFormulaPurpose
1$\text{ROC}_{14} = \frac{P_t - P_{t-14}}{P_{t-14}} \times 100$Long-term rate of change
2$\text{ROC}_{11} = \frac{P_t - P_{t-11}}{P_{t-11}} \times 100$Short-term rate of change
3$\text{Sum} = \text{ROC}_{14} + \text{ROC}_{11}$Combined momentum
4$\text{WMA}_{10} = \frac{\sum_{i=0}^{9}(10-i) \times S_{t-i}}{55}$Weighted smoothing

Real-World Analogy

Think of the Coppock Curve like a grief counselor's assessment of recovery. When a patient (the market) experiences trauma (a crash), they go through stages of grief. The Coppock Curve measures when that mourning period is ending—not when the patient is fully healed, but when they're ready to start living again. The 11-14 month parameters were literally based on how long Episcopal bishops said it takes humans to recover from bereavement.

Why These Values?

Coppock consulted Episcopal bishops to determine the average human mourning period (11-14 months). He theorized market psychology follows similar patterns—investors need time to psychologically recover from bear markets before accumulating again.

7. Practical Cautions

Lag Characteristics

ComponentApproximate Lag
First Valid Value~24-25 bars (months)
Zero-Cross SignalOften 6-12 months after actual bottom
Early Turn Signal3-6 months earlier than zero-cross
Reality Check: Coppock's zero-cross often occurs after 15-30% of the move has already happened. The indicator was designed for *safety*, not early entry. Accept missing the bottom in exchange for higher confidence.

False Signal Scenarios

1. Bear Market Rallies

During extended bear markets, brief upturns can generate false early turn signals.

2. Whipsaws in Secular Bears

2000-2002 and 2008 showed multiple failed signals before the real bottom.

3. Sell Signal Unreliability

Coppock designed the indicator for buying only—sell signals are often too late.

Mitigation Strategies

- Slope Persistence Filter: Require 2+ consecutive positive slope bars

- Price Confirmation: Wait for price to break above 40-week MA

- Use for Buys Only: Ignore sell signals; use other methods to exit

8. Pro Insights

The Slope Entry (Secret Signal)

Professional traders don't wait for the zero-cross. They watch for the slope change while the curve is deeply negative. When the histogram turns from dark red (falling) to light red (rising) while still below zero, this "Early Turn" captures the accumulation phase before the public breakout.

Ignore Sell Signals

Coppock never intended this indicator for selling. Historically, sell signals (crossing below zero) often occur too late, after bear markets have already done their damage. Use the Coppock to get *in*, but use other tools (moving averages, trend lines) to get *out*.

Index Rotation Application

Track Coppock Curves on multiple country indices (SPY, EFA, EEM). When an index's Coppock turns up from deeply negative while others remain negative, it often signals that region will lead the next cycle.

Multi-Timeframe Confirmation

When the monthly Coppock turns positive and the weekly Coppock turns up from oversold, the alignment creates a high-confidence "super signal" for aggressive positioning.

9. Summary

"The Coppock Curve is your market grief counselor—it tells you when the mourning is over and it's time to start accumulating again, not when the party has already started."

PART 2: SUPPLEMENTARY GUIDE

1. Core Concept

The Coppock Curve answers one question: "Is the market psychologically ready to begin a new bull phase?"

Unlike most momentum indicators that react to short-term price changes, the Coppock Curve measures the *cumulative psychological recovery* of market participants after trauma. By summing two long-term rates of change (11 and 14 months), it captures whether the tide of sentiment is genuinely shifting.

Think of it this way:

- ROC components = How much economic/emotional damage occurred

- The sum = Net recovery momentum

- WMA smoothing = Filters out false hopes and dead cat bounces

- The zero line = The psychological threshold between grief and hope

When the curve crosses zero from below, it mathematically confirms that investors have processed the bear market and are ready to buy again.

Why It Works

1. Crowd Psychology Alignment: By modeling human bereavement cycles, it captures deep psychological transitions that technical patterns miss.

2. Noise Filtration: The 10-period WMA eliminates short-term rallies that aren't backed by genuine sentiment change.

3. Asymmetric Design: Optimized for bottoms (fear) rather than tops (greed), matching the asymmetry of market psychology.

Academic Reference

Coppock's original methodology was published in *Barron's* on October 15, 1962. His work was unusual for its sociological grounding—he consulted clergy rather than statisticians to determine parameters.

Psychological Traps

1. The "It's Already Up" Frustration

When Coppock finally signals, you may feel you've missed the move. Remember: The indicator prioritizes *safety* over *timeliness*.

2. Early Turn Overconfidence

Slope turns can fail in extended bears. Always use price confirmation.

3. Applying to Wrong Assets

The indicator was designed for major indices. Using it on individual stocks or crypto produces unreliable results.

Common Misinterpretations

- "Coppock > 0 means keep buying" → It means the recovery is confirmed; may be late stage

- "Use the zero-cross for selling" → Coppock wasn't designed for exits

- "Works on any timeframe" → Monthly is the designed timeframe; daily requires parameter scaling

5. Further Study

Creator Attribution

Edwin Sedgwick Coppock was an economist and founder of Trendex Research in San Antonio, Texas. He was commissioned by the Episcopal Church to identify safe long-term buying opportunities. His unique contribution was applying sociological research (bereavement periods) to market analysis.

Signal Patterns

Classic Buy Signal (Zero Cross)

The zero-line cross is Coppock's original signal, designed to identify the psychological transition from bear market grief to recovery hope. This signal prioritizes safety over timeliness—it often occurs 6-12 months after the actual bottom, but captures 85% of subsequent upside with minimal false signals.


def coppock_buy_signal(coppock, coppock_prev):
    zero_cross = coppock > 0 and coppock_prev <= 0
    return zero_cross

Early Turn Signal (Slope Change)

Professional traders don't wait for the zero-cross. The "early turn" signal triggers when the Coppock Curve is still negative but its slope turns positive—indicating momentum is shifting while still in bear territory. This aggressive entry captures the accumulation phase 3-6 months earlier than the classic signal.


def coppock_early_turn(coppock, coppock_1, coppock_2):
    still_negative = coppock < 0
    slope_positive = coppock > coppock_1
    was_falling = coppock_1 < coppock_2
    
    return still_negative and slope_positive and was_falling

Divergence Signal

Coppock divergences are particularly powerful given the indicator's long-term nature. When price makes a lower low but the Coppock Curve makes a higher low, it signals that the depth of psychological trauma is diminishing despite lower prices—a classic setup for major bottoms.


def coppock_bullish_divergence(price_low, price_prev_low, coppock_low, coppock_prev_low):
    price_lower_low = price_low < price_prev_low
    coppock_higher_low = coppock_low > coppock_prev_low
    
    return price_lower_low and coppock_higher_low
