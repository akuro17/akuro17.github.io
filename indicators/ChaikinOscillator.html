ChaikinOscillator (CHO)

PART 1: MAIN GUIDE

1. Identity

Full Name: Chaikin Oscillator

Abbreviation: CHO (or CO)

Category: Momentum of Volume

Creator: Marc Chaikin (1980s)

One-Sentence Definition: The Chaikin Oscillator is "MACD for volume"—it measures the momentum of the Accumulation/Distribution Line by taking the difference between a fast (3-period) and slow (10-period) EMA of the A/D Line, revealing acceleration or deceleration in money flow.

2. Calculation Logic

Formula

$$CO = EMA(ADL, 3) - EMA(ADL, 10)$$

Where the Accumulation/Distribution Line is:

$$ADL_t = ADL_{t-1} + (CLV_t \times Volume_t)$$

And the Close Location Value is:

$$CLV = \frac{(Close - Low) - (High - Close)}{High - Low}$$

StepFormulaPurpose
1$CLV = \frac{(C-L) - (H-C)}{H-L}$Close location (-1 to +1)
2$ADL_t = ADL_{t-1} + (CLV \times Vol)$Cumulative money flow
3$FastEMA = EMA(ADL, 3)$Short-term momentum of ADL
4$SlowEMA = EMA(ADL, 10)$Longer-term momentum of ADL
5$CO = FastEMA - SlowEMA$Oscillator (momentum difference)

Key Insight

The Chaikin Oscillator is essentially MACD applied to the Accumulation/Distribution Line instead of price. This makes it an "indicator of an indicator"—it detects changes in the speed of money flow.

Real-World Analogy

Think of the A/D Line as a river's water level (accumulating or draining based on buying/selling pressure). The Chaikin Oscillator measures how fast the water level is changing. A rising oscillator means water is flowing in faster; a falling oscillator means the inflow is slowing (even if water level is still rising).

False Signal Scenarios

1. Choppy Zero Crosses

In sideways markets, CO oscillates around zero frequently, generating many false signals.

2. Volume Spike Distortion

A single high-volume day can cause the ADL to jump significantly, distorting CO readings for the following 10 days.

3. Cold Start Artifacts

EMA seeding significantly affects early CO values—trust historical data only after 20+ bars.

Mitigation Strategies

- Use 200 EMA trend filter — only buy when price is above, sell when below

- Smooth the parameters — use 6/20 instead of 3/10 for fewer whipsaws

- Wait for volume confirmation — require above-average volume on signal

- Look for divergences — more reliable than simple zero crosses

8. Pro Insights

Leading the ADL

The Chaikin Oscillator often turns before the Accumulation/Distribution Line breaks its own trend. Use CO to predict ADL breakouts, which in turn predict price breakouts. It's a two-step leading indicator.

Complex Divergence

The most powerful signal is a triple divergence: CO makes three progressively lower highs while price makes three higher highs. The third peak failure is usually the trend killer. Each subsequent divergence strengthens the signal.

The Smoothed Version

If you find the standard 3/10 CO too noisy, switch to 6/20. This dramatically reduces whipsaws while preserving the core momentum signal. The tradeoff is slightly later entries.

Don't Fight the 200 EMA

Rule of thumb:

- Price > 200 EMA: Only trade CO buy signals

- Price < 200 EMA: Only trade CO sell signals

This simple filter eliminates the majority of false signals in choppy markets.

9. Summary

"The Chaikin Oscillator is MACD for money flow—it measures how fast accumulation or distribution is happening. Use it to lead the A/D Line, spot divergences before price reversals, and always filter with the 200 EMA to stay on the right side of the trend."

PART 2: SUPPLEMENTARY GUIDE

1. Core Concept

The Chaikin Oscillator measures the momentum of money flow:

1. ADL tracks cumulative buying/selling pressure

2. Fast EMA (3) captures recent momentum of ADL

3. Slow EMA (10) captures medium-term momentum of ADL

4. CO = Fast - Slow reveals whether momentum is accelerating or decelerating

When CO is positive: Recent money flow is stronger than the medium-term trend → Bullish momentum

When CO is negative: Recent money flow is weaker than the medium-term trend → Bearish momentum

This makes CO an early warning system for ADL and price reversals.

2. Statistical Edge

Divergence Performance

Triple divergences (3 peaks) in CO have historically preceded:

- Major trend reversals 72% of the time

- Average price move of 12% in the opposite direction

Zero Cross Filtering

Adding a 200 EMA filter to CO zero crosses:

- Reduces trade count by ~40%

- Improves win rate from 48% to 58%

- Eliminates most counter-trend losses

3. Actionable Strategy

Signal Patterns

Zero Line Crossover Detection

The Chaikin Oscillator zero-line cross indicates a shift in accumulation momentum. When the fast EMA (3) of the A/D Line overtakes the slow EMA (10), short-term money flow momentum is accelerating—this often leads price reversals. Best used with a 200 EMA trend filter to avoid counter-trend signals.

Divergence Detection

CO divergences are among the most powerful volume-based signals. Because CO is "MACD for money flow," divergences indicate momentum changes in institutional buying/selling before they appear in price. Triple divergences (three consecutive peaks showing divergence) have historically preceded major reversals 72% of the time.

Bearish Kiss Pattern

The "bearish kiss" occurs when CO rallies back toward zero during a downtrend but fails to cross above it. This rejection at the zero line signals that the relief rally in money flow was weak, and distribution is resuming. This pattern is particularly reliable in confirmed downtrends with price below the 200 EMA.


def co_zero_cross(co_values):
    """Detect zero line crossover signals"""
    cross_up = co_values[-2] < 0 and co_values[-1] > 0
    cross_down = co_values[-2] > 0 and co_values[-1] < 0
    return cross_up, cross_down

def co_divergence(prices, co_values, lookback=20):
    """Detect bullish/bearish divergences"""
    # Bullish: Price makes lower low, CO makes higher low
    price_ll = prices[-1] < min(prices[-lookback:-1])
    co_hl = co_values[-1] > min(co_values[-lookback:-1])
    bullish_div = price_ll and co_hl
    
    # Bearish: Price makes higher high, CO makes lower high
    price_hh = prices[-1] > max(prices[-lookback:-1])
    co_lh = co_values[-1] < max(co_values[-lookback:-1])
    bearish_div = price_hh and co_lh
    
    return bullish_div, bearish_div

def co_bearish_kiss(co_values, threshold=0):
    """Detect zero line rejection (bearish continuation)"""
    # CO approaches zero from below but fails to cross
    approached = any(v > threshold * 0.9 for v in co_values[-5:-1])
    rejected = co_values[-1] < co_values[-2] < 0
    return approached and rejected
