Vortex Indicator (VI)

PART 1: MAIN GUIDE

1. Identity

Full Name: Vortex Indicator

Abbreviation: VI (VI+, VI-)

Category: Trend / Directional Movement

Creator: Etienne Botes and Douglas Siepman (2010)

One-Sentence Definition: The Vortex Indicator is a directional trend indicator that identifies the start of a new trend and its direction by measuring the "flow" of upward and downward diagonal price energy.

2. Calculation Logic

Formula

The Vortex Indicator calculates two lines by measuring diagonal price movements normalized by volatility:

Step 1: True Range (TR)

$$TR_t = \max(|High_t - Low_t|, |High_t - Close_{t-1}|, |Low_t - Close_{t-1}|)$$

Step 2: Vortex Movements

$$+VM_t = |High_t - Low_{t-1}|$$

$$-VM_t = |Low_t - High_{t-1}|$$

Step 3: Summation over Period N

$$\sum TR_N = \sum_{i=0}^{N-1} TR_{t-i}$$

$$\sum +VM_N = \sum_{i=0}^{N-1} +VM_{t-i}$$

$$\sum -VM_N = \sum_{i=0}^{N-1} -VM_{t-i}$$

Step 4: Final Indicators

$$VI+ = \frac{\sum +VM_N}{\sum TR_N}$$

$$VI- = \frac{\sum -VM_N}{\sum TR_N}$$

Real-World Analogy

Think of the Vortex Indicator like measuring the strength of two competing currents in a river. The VI+ measures how strongly the water pushes upward (bullish energy), while VI- measures the downward pull (bearish energy). The dominant current determines the river's direction.

7. Practical Cautions

Lag Characteristics

ComponentCharacteristic

Signal ResponsivenessModerate (by design)
Whipsaw Warning: The Vortex Indicator generates frequent false crossovers during consolidation phases. Lines oscillating tightly around 1.0 signal a "no-trade" zone.

False Signal Scenarios

1. Consolidation Chop: VI+ and VI- cross repeatedly without establishing trend

2. Late Entry: Summation lag causes confirmation after significant portion of move

Mitigation Strategies

- Use threshold breakouts (1.10/0.90) instead of simple crosses

- Combine with 50+ period SMA as regime filter

- Wait for spread > 0.20 before committing size

8. Pro Insights

The "Vortex Spread" Concept

Professional traders monitor the gap between VI+ and VI-. A widening spread signals accelerating trend strength; a narrowing spread indicates momentum exhaustion.

Sector Rotation Application

Screen sector ETFs for Vortex crossovers. Allocate capital to sectors where VI+ just crossed above VI- with spread > 0.15.

Volatility Regime Integration

Pair with ATR. When ATR is expanding AND Vortex gives a bullish cross, probability of sustained move increases significantly.

PART 2: SUPPLEMENTARY GUIDE

1. Core Concept

The Vortex Indicator measures diagonal price movements—the distance from the current High to the previous Low (+VM) versus the current Low to the previous High (-VM). These "diagonal energies" represent the bullish impulse pushing prices up from the floor and the bearish impulse pulling prices down from the ceiling.

By normalizing these movements against True Range (volatility), the indicator becomes adaptive—a 10-point move in a low-volatility stock generates the same signal strength as a 100-point move in a high-volatility one.

2. Statistical Edge

Why It Works

The diagonal measurement captures momentum that Close-to-Close methods miss. When a High significantly exceeds the previous Low, it represents genuine buying conviction, not just a gap.

Signal Patterns

Bullish Crossover with Threshold

This signal triggers when VI+ crosses above VI- (indicating bullish momentum dominance) with VI+ exceeding 1.05, confirming strong upward diagonal energy. The additional SMA(200) filter ensures entries are aligned with the long-term trend, reducing false signals in counter-trend environments. When combined with threshold confirmation, this pattern shows 15-20% improved win rates versus simple crossovers.


def vortex_bullish_signal(vi_plus, vi_minus, price, sma200):
    return (
        vi_plus[-1] > vi_minus[-1] and
        vi_plus[-2] <= vi_minus[-2] and  # Cross occurred
        vi_plus[-1] > 1.05 and
        price[-1] > sma200[-1]
    )

Bearish Divergence

This divergence occurs when price makes a higher high while VI+ fails to confirm with a corresponding higher high, signaling weakening upward momentum despite price extension. This momentum failure indicates that the bullish "current" is losing strength and a reversal may be imminent. Divergence signals are particularly reliable when occurring after extended uptrends with VI+ previously above 1.10.


def vortex_bearish_divergence(price, vi_plus):
    price_hh = price[-1] > max(price[-10:-1])  # Price Higher High
    vi_lh = vi_plus[-1] < max(vi_plus[-10:-1])  # VI+ Lower High
    return price_hh and vi_lh
