BollingerBands (BB)

PART 1: MAIN GUIDE

1. Identity

Full Name: Bollinger Bands

Abbreviation: BB

Category: Volatility Overlay

Creator: John Bollinger (1980s)

One-Sentence Definition: Bollinger Bands are a volatility-adaptive envelope around price that defines what is statistically "high" or "low" on a relative basis, telling you when markets are stretched or compressed.

2. Calculation Logic

Formula

$$\text{Middle Band (MB)} = SMA(\text{Close}, n)$$

$$\text{Upper Band (UB)} = MB + (k \times \sigma)$$

$$\text{Lower Band (LB)} = MB - (k \times \sigma)$$

StepFormulaPurpose
1$SMA = \frac{\sum_{i=1}^{n} Close_i}{n}$Calculate 20-period average (Middle Band)
2$\sigma = \sqrt{\frac{\sum (Close_i - SMA)^2}{n}}$Standard deviation of closes
3$UB = SMA + (2 \times \sigma)$Upper Band (2 std devs above)
4$LB = SMA - (2 \times \sigma)$Lower Band (2 std devs below)

Why Standard Deviation?

Standard deviation dynamically adapts to market volatility. In calm markets, bands contract (squeeze). In volatile markets, bands expand. This self-adjusting mechanism makes Bollinger Bands relevant across all market conditions.

Real-World Analogy

Think of Bollinger Bands as elastic guardrails on a highway. In calm traffic (low volatility), the guardrails sit close to the road center. In chaotic conditions (high volatility), they widen to accommodate larger swings. When price "crashes" into the guardrails, it often bounces back toward the center.

7. Practical Cautions

Lag Characteristics

ComponentNotes

Squeeze DetectionObservable only after contraction occurs

False Signal Scenarios

1. The Head Fake

During a Squeeze, price briefly breaks one band then immediately reverses to break the opposite band. The first move is a trap.

2. Band Walking

In strong uptrends, price can "walk" along the upper band for weeks. Each touch is NOT a sell signal—it's confirmation of trend strength.

3. Flatline Collapse

If price remains constant (halted stock, weekend gaps), σ = 0 and all bands converge to a single line.

Mitigation Strategies

- Wait for candlestick confirmation at band touches before trading

- Use ADX filter to distinguish trends from ranges

- Combine with volume — breakouts need volume expansion to be valid

- Watch for "head fakes" — the second move is often the real one

8. Pro Insights

The Squeeze Is the Setup

Professional traders watch for Bollinger Band Width to reach historically low levels (bottom 5% of 6-month range). This "squeeze" indicates that a major move is coming—direction unknown. They position with breakout stops above and below the range.

Band Walking = Trend Strength

When price "walks" the upper band—making new highs while repeatedly touching the band—do NOT fade the move. This indicates strong bullish momentum. The band is acting as dynamic support, not resistance.

The Head Fake Rule

During squeezes, the initial breakout direction is often wrong. Wait for a decisive daily close outside the band, or look for the first break to fail and trade the reversal.

Volatility Cycles

Markets cycle between expansion and contraction. Wide bands eventually contract; narrow bands eventually expand. Trade the extreme to the mean.

9. Summary

"Bollinger Bands tell you when the market is stretched (extremes at the bands) or coiled (squeeze near the middle)—trade the bounce in ranges, ride the break in trends, and always respect the band walk in strong momentum."

PART 2: SUPPLEMENTARY GUIDE

1. Core Concept

Bollinger Bands answer the question: "Is price high or low on a relative basis?"

Unlike fixed price levels, the bands adjust to volatility. When markets are calm, "high" and "low" are defined narrowly. When markets are wild, those definitions widen.

The Middle Band (SMA) acts as the trend anchor:

- Price above → Bullish regime

- Price below → Bearish regime

The Outer Bands define statistical extremes:

- Price at Upper Band → Stretched to the upside

- Price at Lower Band → Stretched to the downside

Squeeze Performance

Historical backtests show that after a Bollinger Band Squeeze (bottom 10% of 6-month Bandwidth), the subsequent 20-day move averages 2.5x larger than normal periods.

Signal Patterns

Squeeze Breakout Detection

When Bollinger Bandwidth contracts to less than half its average (indicating compressed volatility) and price simultaneously closes beyond the outer band, a squeeze breakout has occurred. This "coiled spring" pattern historically produces moves 2.5× larger than normal, making it one of the highest-probability setups. The challenge is direction prediction, which requires additional confirmation.


def bb_squeeze_breakout(close, upper, lower, middle, bandwidth, avg_bw):
    """Detect squeeze breakout signals"""
    squeeze = bandwidth < 0.5 * avg_bw
    breakout_up = close > upper and squeeze
    breakout_down = close < lower and squeeze
    return breakout_up, breakout_down

W-Bottom Reversal Pattern

The W-Bottom is a powerful bullish reversal pattern where price makes two lows: the first touches or breaks the lower band, followed by a rally, then a second low that holds above the lower band (higher relative low). When price breaks above the intervening rally high, the pattern confirms. This formation suggests that sellers are exhausting and buyers are stepping in at higher prices.

Middle Band Crossover Detection

The Middle Band (20-period SMA) acts as a dynamic trend filter. When price crosses above the Middle Band from below, it signals a potential shift to bullish momentum. Crossing below signals bearish momentum. In trending markets, the Middle Band often acts as support (uptrend) or resistance (downtrend) on pullbacks.


def bb_middle_cross(close, middle):
    """Middle band crossover signals"""
    cross_up = close[-2] < middle[-2] and close[-1] > middle[-1]
    cross_down = close[-2] > middle[-2] and close[-1] < middle[-1]
    return cross_up, cross_down
