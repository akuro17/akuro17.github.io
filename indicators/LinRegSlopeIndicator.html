Linear Regression Slope

PART 1: MAIN GUIDE

1. Identity

Full Name: Linear Regression Slope Indicator

Abbreviation: LRS / LinReg Slope

Category: Momentum Oscillator

Creator: Statistical derivation

One-Sentence Definition: The Linear Regression Slope measures the rate of change (gradient) of the best-fit regression line over a period, quantifying both the direction and intensity of the prevailing trend.

2. Calculation Logic

Formula

The slope is the $m$ coefficient from the linear regression equation $y = mx + b$:

$$Slope = \frac{N \sum(xy) - \sum x \sum y}{N \sum(x^2) - (\sum x)^2}$$

VariableDefinition
$N$Period (lookback length)
$x$Time index (0, 1, 2, ... N-1)
$y$Closing price

Interpretation

- Positive Slope: Price is rising on average over the period

- Negative Slope: Price is falling on average over the period

- Higher Absolute Value: Steeper trend (faster price change)

- Near Zero: Flat/no trend

Real-World Analogy

Think of the slope like the speedometer of a car's direction. If you're driving north at 60 mph, the slope is +60. If south at 30 mph, it's -30. The magnitude tells you how fast the car is moving; the sign tells you the direction. Unlike momentum (which just compares two prices), slope considers *all* prices in the period, giving a smoothed measure of trend velocity.

7. Practical Cautions

False Signal Scenarios

1. Low R-Squared Trap

Slope is positive but R² is 0.05—the "trend" is random scatter.

2. Price Level Distortion

BTC slope might be 500; EUR/USD slope might be 0.0002—can't compare directly.

3. Noise Oscillation

In choppy markets, slope crosses zero frequently without meaningful trend.

Mitigation Strategies

- R-Squared Filter: Only trade when R² > 0.20

- Normalize Slope: Convert to Z-score or percentage for cross-asset comparison

- Smooth Output: Apply 3-period SMA to reduce jitter

8. Pro Insights

The R² Filter is Mandatory

Slope without R-squared is dangerous. A slope can be calculated on any data—even random noise. R² tells you if the data actually fits a line. If R² < 0.15, the slope value is statistically meaningless.

Divergence with Price

The slope is the *first derivative* of the trend. If price makes a new high but slope is declining, the trend is decelerating—a classic bearish divergence. This often appears 2-5 bars before price tops.

9. Summary

"Linear Regression Slope is the trend's velocity—positive means upward motion, the magnitude shows how fast, and a hook warns that brakes are coming."

PART 2: SUPPLEMENTARY GUIDE

1. Core Concept

The slope answers: "How fast is price moving in this direction?"

While LSMA gives you the *endpoint* of the regression line (price level), the slope gives you the *gradient*—how steeply the line is tilted. A flat line has slope near 0; a steep upward line has large positive slope.

2. Statistical Edge

The edge comes from seeing *acceleration* before *peak*. In physics, velocity peaks before position. Similarly, slope (trend velocity) often peaks and hooks down while price is still making new highs.

3. Actionable Strategy

Strategy Name: "Slope Zero Cross"

Setup: Slope recently negative (downtrend ending)

Entry: Slope crosses above 0 with R² > 0.25

Stop: Below recent swing low

Exit: Slope crosses below 0

4. Risks & Limitations

- Non-Normalized: Values vary by asset price level

- Noise Susceptible: Oscillates in range-bound markets

- R² Dependency: Useless without R² validation

5. Further Study

Parent/Child: LSMA (slope is its derivative), R-Squared (validation metric)

PART 3: ADVANCED SIGNAL REFERENCE

Signal Patterns

Slope Zero Cross Signal

This signal fires when the Linear Regression Slope crosses above zero with R-squared validation. The zero cross indicates the best-fit trend direction has shifted from falling to rising. The R² filter is mandatory—slope calculated on random data is meaningless. When R² > 0.20, the slope has statistical significance.


def slope_buy_signal(slope, slope_prev, r_squared):
    zero_cross = slope > 0 and slope_prev <= 0
    valid_fit = r_squared > 0.20
    return zero_cross and valid_fit

Screening Filters


LinRegSlope(20) > 0
AND LinRegSlope(20)[1] <= 0  // Zero cross today
AND RSquared(20) > 0.25
