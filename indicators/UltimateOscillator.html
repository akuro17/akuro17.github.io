Ultimate Oscillator (UO)

PART 1: MAIN GUIDE

1. Identity

Full Name: Ultimate Oscillator

Abbreviation: UO

Category: Momentum Oscillator

Creator: Larry Williams (1976, published 1985)

One-Sentence Definition: The Ultimate Oscillator is a multi-timeframe momentum indicator that combines buying pressure across three different periods to capture trend momentum while avoiding the premature saturation common in single-period oscillators.

2. Calculation Logic

Formula

$$UO = 100 \times \frac{(4 \times Avg_7) + (2 \times Avg_{14}) + (1 \times Avg_{28})}{4 + 2 + 1}$$

Where each $Avg_N$ is:

$$Avg_N = \frac{\sum_{i=1}^{N} BP_i}{\sum_{i=1}^{N} TR_i}$$

Component Definitions

ComponentFormulaPurpose
Buying Pressure (BP)$Close - \min(Low, PriorClose)$Distance bulls pushed price from session's true low
True Range (TR)$\max(High, PriorClose) - \min(Low, PriorClose)$Total range including gap

Weighting Logic

TimeframePeriodWeightEmphasis
Short-term74Highest (most responsive)
Intermediate142Moderate
Long-term281Lowest (most stable)

The denominator (4+2+1=7) normalizes the result to the 0-100 scale.

Real-World Analogy

Think of UO like a three-speed weather station. The short-term sensor (7) detects hour-by-hour changes, the medium-term (14) tracks daily patterns, and the long-term (28) measures seasonal trends. By weighting and combining all three, you get a forecast that's responsive to current conditions but anchored to the bigger picture.

Why These Values?

Larry Williams selected 7, 14, 28 to represent approximately one week, two weeks, and one month of market activity. This multi-timeframe approach prevents the UO from staying pinned at extremes during sustained trends—a flaw common in single-period oscillators like RSI.

7. Practical Cautions

Lag Characteristics

ComponentCharacteristic
First Valid ValueMax(Period1, Period2, Period3) + 1 = 29 bars
Signal DelayModerate (multi-timeframe smoothing)
Divergence ConfirmationRequires additional 2-5 bars after divergence forms
Williams' Warning: Larry Williams emphasized that the breakout rule is mandatory. Buying simply because UO < 30 is dangerous—you must wait for the oscillator to break the divergence swing high.

False Signal Scenarios

1. Premature Divergence Entry

Divergence can persist for weeks while price grinds lower. Never enter on divergence alone—wait for the breakout confirmation.

2. Waterfall Crash Override

In panic selloffs, UO can stay oversold for extended periods. The multi-timeframe weighting helps but doesn't eliminate this risk.

3. The 50-Line Chop

When UO oscillates around 50 in a range, centerline crosses are meaningless. Require UO to reach extremes first.

Mitigation Strategies

- Breakout Rule Mandatory: Never trade divergence without the swing high/low breakout

- RSI Confirmation: Require RSI to also show divergence for higher probability

- Price Level Confluence: Only trade UO signals at key support/resistance levels

8. Pro Insights

The 50-Line Filter

While Williams focused on divergences, the 50 centerline is a powerful regime filter. If UO > 50, the weighted buying pressure exceeds selling pressure across all three timeframes—a bullish bias. Many traders refuse to short when UO > 50, regardless of other signals.

The Depth of Oversold

Not all oversold readings are equal. Williams noted that:

- UO dropping to 28-30 creates the best subsequent rallies

- UO crashing to 10-15 often indicates "waterfall" selling with more downside ahead

- The ideal buy zone is a shallow oversold dip (25-35), not a crash

Trendline Magic

Draw trendlines directly on the UO oscillator, not just price. A trendline break on UO often precedes the price trendline break by 2-5 bars, providing an early warning of momentum shift.

Multi-Asset Divergence Scan

Scan for UO divergences across sectors:

1. Sector ETF price makes new low

2. Sector ETF's UO makes higher low (divergence)

3. Lead stocks in sector showing same divergence

This triple-divergence setup has historically preceded sector rotation bottoms.

9. Summary

"The Ultimate Oscillator is your multi-focal lens—seeing momentum through three timeframes prevents the tunnel vision that traps single-period traders."

PART 2: SUPPLEMENTARY GUIDE

1. Core Concept

The Ultimate Oscillator solves a fundamental flaw in traditional oscillators: premature saturation.

Consider RSI(14): in a strong uptrend, RSI quickly rises above 70 (overbought) and stays there while price doubles. Traders who sold at "overbought" missed the entire move. The problem? RSI only looks at one timeframe.

Williams' insight: momentum manifests differently across timeframes. A market might be overbought on a 7-day basis yet just beginning to trend on a 28-day basis. By combining all three views with intelligent weighting, UO creates a more complete picture:

- Short-term (7) — What's happening right now?

- Medium-term (14) — What's the near-term trend?

- Long-term (28) — What's the big-picture direction?

The heavy weighting on short-term (4×) ensures responsiveness, while the inclusion of longer terms (2× and 1×) prevents the indicator from permanently saturating during sustained moves.

3. Actionable Strategy

Strategy Name: "Williams' Divergence Breakout"

Concept: Trade confirmed divergences using the exact method Larry Williams prescribed.

Indicators Required:

- Ultimate Oscillator (7, 14, 28)

- Price chart with swing detection

Setup Requirements (Long):

1. Price makes a Lower Low

2. UO makes a Higher Low (bullish divergence)

3. UO is below 30 at the divergence low

Entry Trigger:

- Identify the highest UO peak between the two divergence lows

- Wait for UO to rise above this peak level

Position Sizing:

- Full size: Divergence occurred with UO < 25

- Half size: Divergence occurred with UO 25-35

Exit Rules:

1. Profit Target: Previous swing high

2. Stop Loss: Below the divergence low (2× ATR max)

3. Time Exit: 20 bars without reaching target

Example:


Stock XYZ shows:
  Price Low #1 at $48, UO = 25
  Price rallies, UO peaks at 45
  Price Low #2 at $46 (Lower Low), UO = 28 (Higher Low) ✓
  Divergence confirmed, UO was below 30 ✓

Entry Trigger: Wait for UO > 45 (the swing high peak)
  UO rises to 47 → ENTRY at current price $50

Stop: Below $46 (divergence low) = $45.50
Target: Previous high at $54

Risk: $4.50, Reward: $4.00, RR = 0.9:1
(Note: Typical divergence trades have higher RR due to momentum continuation)

Psychological Traps

1. The "It's Oversold" Trap

Seeing UO < 30 feels like a guaranteed buy. But oversold can become more oversold. The breakout rule exists for a reason.

2. Divergence Impatience

Divergences can take 10-20 bars to resolve. Trading too early turns high-probability setups into coin flips.

3. Ignoring the 50 Line

The centerline tells you who's in control. Trading against the 50-line position fights the aggregate momentum.

Common Misinterpretations

- "UO < 30 is a buy signal" → It's a setup, not a trigger; wait for breakout

- "Higher = more bullish" → UO measures momentum, not price level

- "Works in all markets" → Trends can override even multi-timeframe oscillators

Signal Patterns

Buy Signal (Williams Divergence Method)

Larry Williams' original UO trading method requires both divergence AND breakout confirmation—never trading divergence alone. The pattern first detects bullish divergence (price making lower low while UO makes higher low in oversold territory), then waits for UO to break above the swing high between the divergence lows. This two-step confirmation improves win rate from 55% (divergence alone) to 75% (divergence + breakout).


def uo_buy_signal(price_lows, uo_lows, uo_swing_high, current_uo):
    # Step 1: Bullish divergence
    price_lower_low = price_lows[-1] < price_lows[-2]
    uo_higher_low = uo_lows[-1] > uo_lows[-2]
    uo_oversold = uo_lows[-1] < 30
    
    divergence = price_lower_low and uo_higher_low and uo_oversold
    
    # Step 2: Breakout confirmation
    breakout = current_uo > uo_swing_high
    
    return divergence and breakout

Sell Signal (Williams Divergence Method)

The bearish counterpart requires price making higher highs while UO makes lower highs above 70 (overbought), followed by UO breaking below the swing low between divergence peaks. This methodical approach avoids the common mistake of selling simply because UO is overbought—strong trends can maintain overbought readings for extended periods. The breakdown confirmation ensures counter-trend moves have genuine conviction.


def uo_sell_signal(price_highs, uo_highs, uo_swing_low, current_uo):
    # Step 1: Bearish divergence
    price_higher_high = price_highs[-1] > price_highs[-2]
    uo_lower_high = uo_highs[-1] < uo_highs[-2]
    uo_overbought = uo_highs[-1] > 70
    
    divergence = price_higher_high and uo_lower_high and uo_overbought
    
    # Step 2: Breakdown confirmation
    breakdown = current_uo < uo_swing_low
    
    return divergence and breakdown

Centerline Trend Filter

While Williams focused on divergences, the 50-centerline serves as a powerful regime filter. When UO exceeds 50, weighted buying pressure dominates selling pressure across all three timeframes (7, 14, 28)—indicating aggregate bullish control. Many traders refuse to short when UO > 50, using this as a hard filter regardless of other signals.


def uo_trend_filter(uo_value):
    if uo_value > 50:
        return "BULLISH_BIAS"
    elif uo_value < 50:
        return "BEARISH_BIAS"
    else:
        return "NEUTRAL"

Divergence Setup Scanner


SET UO = UltimateOscillator(7, 14, 28)
SET Price_Low_20 = Lowest(Low, 20)
SET UO_Low_20 = Lowest(UO, 20)

FILTER WHERE:
